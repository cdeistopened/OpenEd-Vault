## THE 8-YEAR EXPERIMENT

A kid started piano at eight. Mediocre. Switched to guitar. Mediocre. Ukulele. Back to piano. Eight years of showing up, paying for lessons, and not practicing.

Then at 16 he walked in and said: "I bought a bass."

Something clicked. He practiced hours daily. Found his thing.

Deborah Pratt runs a music school with 150+ students. She shared this story on [our podcast this week](https://opened.co/blog?type=podcast) and called it the Practice Paradox.

"If I had been on him about practicing, or if his parents had required practice to have lessons, he would never have reached this."

He needed music to exist in his life long enough for him to grow into it.

Most parents have patience backwards. Patient with actions (skipping practice), impatient with results (why aren't you better?). Flip it. Be impatient with actions - keep showing up. Be patient with results - it took this kid eight years.

## TOOL: HOFFMAN ACADEMY

If you're in the practice wars, [Hoffman Academy](https://www.hoffmanacademy.com/) came up repeatedly in our research. Video-based piano lessons that remove you as the enforcer.
