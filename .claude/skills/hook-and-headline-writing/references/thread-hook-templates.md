# Thread Hook Templates

20+ proven templates for social media thread openers (X/Twitter, LinkedIn, Facebook).

## How to Use This Guide

1. **Match content type to template category**
2. **Fill in the blanks** with your specific content
3. **Adjust voice** for platform (LinkedIn = professional, X = punchy, Facebook = casual)
4. **Test 3-5 variations** from different categories
5. **Track which categories perform best** for your audience

---

## Template Categories

### Category 1: Expertise & Authority
Best for: Sharing experience, lessons learned, industry insights

### Category 2: Transformation Stories
Best for: Before/after journeys, achievement showcases, personal growth

### Category 3: Value Lists & Resources
Best for: Tools, tips, frameworks, curated recommendations

### Category 4: Bold Claims & Pattern Interrupts
Best for: Counterintuitive insights, challenging assumptions

### Category 5: Problem-Focused
Best for: Addressing pain points, mistakes, obstacles

### Category 6: Behind the Scenes
Best for: Process reveals, strategy breakdowns, "how I did it"

## CATEGORY 1: Expertise & Authority Templates

### Template 1: Expert Knowledge Share

**Formula:**
```
[X years/experiences] in [field].

Here's what every [target audience] should know:
```

**Examples:**

*General:*
"10 years in SEO.

Here's what every new digital marketer should know:"

*OpenEd:*
"15 years homeschooling 4 kids.

Here's what every new homeschool parent should know:"

"5 years running a microschool.

**When to use:**
- You have significant experience
- Audience respects years/credentials
- Educational content
- LinkedIn, X/Twitter

- LinkedIn: Use professional title
- X: Keep punchy, use shorter timeframe
- Facebook: More conversational tone

## Platform-Specific Adaptations

### For X/Twitter:
- Keep sentences short and punchy
- Use emojis sparingly (ðŸ§µ for threads is standard)
- First tweet must hook in <280 characters
- Test both long-form first tweet vs short hook

### For LinkedIn:
- Can be longer and more detailed
- Professional tone (but still conversational)
- First 2 lines show in feed (hook must work there)
- Use line breaks for readability
- Hashtags less important (3-5 max)

### For Facebook:
- More casual, personal tone
- Question-based hooks perform well
- Community/relationship focus
- NO hashtags (Facebook doesn't reward them)
- Works well with visual (post image question)
