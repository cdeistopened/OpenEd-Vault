# Day-in-the-Life Blog Post Format

## Overview

A Day-in-the-Life post shows how a specific homeschool family structures their learning, with enough detail that readers can visualize themselves doing it. It's part memoir, part practical guide, and ends with a concrete inventory of tools and resources.

## Structure

### 1. Opening Hook + Philosophy Statement
- Start with something counterintuitive or specific about their approach
- State the educational philosophy in 1-2 sentences
- Example: "Our school day doesn't start until 10:00 AM. We spend weeks studying 'Pet Adoption' or 'Bigfoot.' This isn't a compromise. It's Open Educationâ€”and it's completely intentional."

### 2. The "Controversial" Choice Section
- Address the thing that would raise eyebrows in traditional schooling
- Explain why they chose it and what they were escaping
- Frame it as intentional, not lazy
- Example: Late starts, no grades, screen time, unschooling elements, etc.

### 3. The Core Approach (Their "Secret Weapon")
- What makes their homeschool unique
- Specific methodology or curriculum philosophy
- Include concrete examples with names, tools, and anecdotes
- Show flexibility and pivots

### 4. The Tech Stack / Village Section
- What they can't or won't teach themselves
- How OpenEd funding enables this
- List of specific tools, classes, and outside resources
- The "village" of people who help

### 5. The "Master Plan + Life Happens" Section
- How they plan the year/week
- A specific example of when they threw out the plan
- Frame flexibility as feature, not bug

### 6. Closing Line
- Return to the philosophy
- Something memorable and quotable

### 7. Inventory Section
- Organized by category (Planning, Core Curriculum, Enrichment, etc.)
- Each item includes: Name, link, brief description, how they use it
- Mention what's OpenEd reimbursable where relevant

---

## Tone & Voice

- First person, conversational
- Specific details (times, names, anecdotes)
- Honest about struggles (not aspirational Instagram homeschool)
- Permission-giving (validates unconventional choices)
- Practical (readers should be able to copy elements)

---

## Example Categories for Inventory

**Planning & AI Tools**
- Perplexity, ChatGPT, Google Sheets, Skylight Calendar, etc.

**Core Curriculum**
- Math: Teaching Textbooks, Beast Academy, Synthesis, etc.
- Language Arts: Write Shop, All About Reading, etc.
- Science/History: Unit studies, Campfire Curriculums, etc.

**The "Village" / Enrichment**
- OpenEd classes (online and in-person)
- Co-ops, tutors, community classes
- Local resources (museums, libraries, etc.)

**Media & Inspiration**
- Movies, documentaries, podcasts used in learning

---

## Length

- Main narrative: 800-1,200 words
- Inventory: 300-500 words (list format)
- Total: ~1,200-1,700 words

---

## What Makes It Work

1. **Specificity**: Real names of curricula, real times, real anecdotes
2. **Permission**: Validates choices that might feel "wrong" in traditional schooling
3. **Practicality**: Readers can implement pieces immediately
4. **Personality**: The family's unique voice and values come through
5. **Inventory**: Concrete takeaways they can shop/research
