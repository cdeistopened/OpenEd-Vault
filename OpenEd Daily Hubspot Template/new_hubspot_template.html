Education policy shifts

Recent shifts in education policy are emphasizing a return to foundational skills. After a decade of experimentation with new teaching methodologies, many districts are finding that traditional approaches to reading and mathematics instruction produce more consistent results. This doesn't mean abandoning innovation, but rather ensuring that new methods are built upon proven foundations.

Charter school enrollment has increased by 7% nationwide over the past year, continuing a five-year growth trend. Parents cite academic performance, specialized curricula, and smaller class sizes as primary motivators for the switch.
