What's the difference between students who "get" math and those who don't? Often it's just whether someone explained why things work, not just how.

When one teacher stopped teaching the "keep, change, flip" trick for dividing fractions and started using actual cookies to show why it works, over 80% of her students mastered the concept.

Maybe we're not divided into "math people" and "non-math people." We're just divided into those who were shown the concepts and those who were only taught the tricks.
