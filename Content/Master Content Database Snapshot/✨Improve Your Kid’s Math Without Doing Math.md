### ==‚ú®What if I told you, you could help your child get better at math - without doing math?==

==Not only that - but it‚Äôd be delightful.==

### ==To your brain, math is visual.==

==You read that right ‚Üí VISUAL. Technically, it‚Äôs visuospatial.==

==Let me explain.==

==Visual is processing images or patterns; while spatial means handling locations or sequences. Together, this means that your brain thinks of math as objects or patterns in different places or sequences.==

==Math savants will tell you that they ‚Äúsee‚Äù the math. They universally use this term. Moreover, there are some people with specialized brains that can look at objects on the floor and count them instantaneously into the hundreds.==

==Most of you aren‚Äôt math savants (I‚Äôm not either). You also probably don‚Äôt think you ‚Äúsee‚Äù math, but you do. How many birds are in the picture below?==

==Did you count them 1, 2, 3 - or did you just== _==know==_ ==there were 3. Most of us can only do this trick up to 5, but you can still easily look at the picture below and tell me which has more tulips. You didn‚Äôt count them, you visualized them spatially and just== _==knew.==_

==The parts of your brain that manage visual information evolved into the parts of your brain that manage math - because math is so physical.==

==Your brain also stores numbers spatially. I want you to imagine the numbers 5 and 50. Now put the 5 in one hand and the 50 in the other. Most of you put the 5 in your left hand and the 50 in your right hand. That‚Äôs because you have a spatial representation of numbers in your mind.==

==Cool right? Now, if you were raised in a culture that writes right-to-left, you put the 5 in your right hand. This means that the direction of spatial orientation is not innate, but trained - but spatial orientation exists in everyone‚Äôs mind regardless of culture.==

### ==You‚Äôre probably thinking that‚Äôs cool Claire but why should I care about this?==

==You should care because children‚Äôs visuospatial working memory - the ability to hold and manipulate visual and spatial information - is== _==highly==_ ==correlated to math ability.==

==It‚Äôs not just related to the ability to do numerical operations (think addition/subtraction) but also math reasoning (think story problems).==

### ==Visuospatial working memory predicts math ability from preschool to high school, and even mathematical reasoning and geometry in adults.==

==Lack of these skills also predicts math difficulty - including dyscalculia.==

_==Side note:==_ ==Dyscalculia and dyslexia commonly occur together so if you have a child with dyslexia it‚Äôs worth getting them tested for dyscalculia too.==

==But most importantlyüëá==

### ==Training visuospatial tasks improves math ability!==

==Not only that, the training is delightful!==

==But, not all visuospatial tasks are created equal. First, let‚Äôs talk about the ones you need to train, and then I‚Äôll give you a handy list - by age - to help your kids crush math.==

### ==There are two types of visuospatial tasks that are particularly important to math skills.==

- ==Visuospatial manipulation ‚Üí Examples: Tetris, using an upside down map, navigating a parking lot==
- ==Spatial-Sequencing ‚Üí Examples: Building/construction, dance choreography, sports==

==These two categories work together - manipulation builds the== _==mental models==_ ==of space, and sequencing keeps them in== _==order==_==. Train both, and you‚Äôre giving your child the mental architecture math thrives on.==

### ==Kids who can picture things in their minds and remember the order of steps have a big advantage in math.==

==These ‚Äúmind‚Äôs eye‚Äù skills make it easier to understand shapes, patterns, and how things fit together. The good news? You can grow these skills through simple, playful activities‚Äîno worksheets required. Here‚Äôs an guide for kids, aged 3-18, you can use at home==

### ==How to Use This List==

==This list is== ==**long on purpose**====‚Äînot because you should do everything, but so you have plenty of choices to fit your child‚Äôs age, personality, and what you already have at home.==

==You will notice that the activities for ages 3-5 are super common in most households, but these activities are rarely extended to the upper ages, and unless kids have a unique interest in STEM are often are entirely eliminated in the middle/high school years. Thus, take the time to read how these activities grow and change as your child ages. In the younger ages, focus on breadth, in the older ages focus on depth ‚Üí let them find something they want to work deeply on.==

==I know it doesn‚Äôt seem right but letting your kid become great at origami or model planes will help them be better at math!==

==**FINAL NOTE**====: To properly train visuospatial working memory, ideally kids are doing mental rotations. I‚Äôve suggested how to add that to activities below.==

==‚ú®Ages 3‚Äì5: Foundational Skills==

---

==**Why it matters:**== ==At this age, kids are wiring their brains to notice shapes, positions, and short sequences‚Äîcore building blocks for future math reasoning.==

### ==üß© Hands-on Building & Creating (Visuospatial Manipulation)==

- ==**Wooden Blocks, Magnetic Tiles, Playdough Legos**== ==‚Äì Let children make their own. Visuospatial challenge‚Üí make your own objects, ask if your child to see if they can copy you. Then, do mental rotations. Ask them ‚Äúcan you make it upside down‚Äù or ‚Äúon its side‚Äù==
- ==**Simple Puzzles (24 pcs)**== ==- Puzzles of increasing pieces great for all ages - these naturally include mental rotations ‚Üí as kids age 3D puzzles are great.==
- ==**Stacking Cups/Rings**== ==‚Äì Stack, flip one, restack - the more alternative methods the better.==
- ==**Shape Sorters**== ==‚Äì this is for ages 2-3 typically, but you can make your own shape sorters for older kids with objects that have rotational symmetry which is harder but super fun! The more complex the objects the better.==
- ==**Sandcastle Building**== ==‚Äì Same rules as above, kid makes, kid copies, then "upside down‚Äù or ‚Äúon it‚Äôs side‚Äù==
- ==**Train Tracks**== ==- This costs money, but when you purchase find ones that have up/down or unusual connections. Better buy from multiple different brands and see how the kids put them together.==
- ==**Mirror Play**== ==‚Äì This is a fun addition to all the above. Can you build ‚Äúmirror objects‚Äù. Use a mirror to help.==
- ==**Sensory Bin**== ==‚Äì Hide shapes and objects, rotate them before revealing.==

### ==üîÑ Memory for Steps & Patterns (Spatial Sequencing)==

==Strengthens remembering and ordering multi-step math processes.==

- ==**Simon Says Steps**== ==‚Äì 2‚Äì3 spatial moves (‚Äúleft, forward, spin‚Äù).==
- ==**Obstacle Course Recall**== ==‚Äì Navigate 3‚Äì4 moves, repeat.==
- ==**Clapping Patterns**== ==‚Äì Clap short rhythms, repeat exactly.==
- ==**Beginner Dance Routines**== ==‚Äì 3‚Äì4 moves, recall order.==
- ==**Bead/Block Patterns (necklaces, anklets)**== ==‚Äì Arrange 3‚Äì4, mix, rebuild from memory.==
- ==**Memory Matching Games**== ==‚Äì Flip 3‚Äì4 cards, recall order.==
- ==**Animal Movement Sequences**== ==‚Äì Hop, crawl, spin‚Äîrecall order.==
- ==**Musical Chairs with Moves**== ==‚Äì Add actions to each round.==
- ==**Echo Clapping with Twist**== ==‚Äì Repeat, add one motion.==
- ==**Toy Car Path**== ==‚Äì Trace a path, remember and retrace.==
- ==**Story Action Sequences**== ==‚Äì Act out steps from a story.==

==‚ú®Ages 6‚Äì9: Skill Expansion==

---

==**Main Goal:**== ==Strengthen mental rotation and working memory for more complex problem-solving.==

### ==üß© Hands-on Building & Creating (Visuospatial Manipulation)==

==Supports visualizing multi-step math problems.==

- ==**Lego Builds**== ==‚Äì Create small builds from memory. Let them make the same object more than once - let them make their own objects. Also, see if they can make a mirror of the object - you can use a literal mirror to help them do this.==
- ==**Origami**== ==‚Äì This can get quite complex and requires a lot of mental rotations when using instructions.==
- ==**Tangram Puzzles**== ==‚Äì Build designs from outlines, then recreate rotational symmetric objects==
- ==**Snap Circuits**== ==‚Äì Assemble simple circuits from diagrams, recall after viewing. Snap circuits are also a super fun for your engineering-focused kids!==
- ==**3D Model Kits**== ==‚Äì Assemble and reassemble! As kids get older, see if they can start to guess where things go. It‚Äôs about learning how to do these WITHOUT instructions.==
- ==**Cube Puzzles**== ==‚Äì Solve after imagining 1‚Äì2 rotations.== _==(Rubik‚Äôs-type - but there are other choices)==_ ==Canoodle is a great choice and inexpensive. Tetris is also VERY good.==
- ==**Magnetic Marble Runs**== ==‚Äì Build, mentally swap sections, rebuild.==
- ==**Pattern Block Designs**== ==‚Äì Copy patterns from memory. You can get create and do mosaic art. These can get quite complex. Watch== ==[THIS](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b9IFK8i0iSM)== ==video on this incredible glass mosaics and then have your kids try to figure out how to create their own.==
- ==**Construction Straws**== ==‚Äì Build shapes from diagrams without looking.==

### ==üîÑ Memory for Steps & Patterns (Spatial Sequencing)==

==Improves mental ordering of longer processes.==

- ==**Pattern Replication**== ==‚Äì Copy color/shape patterns from memory.==
- ==**Drawing from Memory**== ==‚Äì Having them draw from their environment is great for visuospatial, but it‚Äôs even better if they can start trying to do it from memory too!==
- ==**Clay Replicas**== ==‚Äì Same rules as above - learn to do it with observation first, then through memory.==
- ==**Card Flip Order**== ==‚Äì Recall order of 5 flipped cards. This one is kinda contrived, but think about games like mahjong or blackjack where ‚Äúcounting‚Äù cards is helpful to winning.==
- ==**Mini Chess Challenges**== ==‚Äì Having them memorize chess patterns of expert players and then figuring out when to use them in game play will be very helpful.==
- ==**Marble Maze Recall**== ==‚Äì Navigate a maze, then repeat path. Have them start making their own mazes.==
- ==**Step-by-Step Crafts**== ==‚Äì Have them learn their own crafts - knitting more complex items - hats, gloves, or learning to make things out of wood.==
- ==**Cooking Step Recall**== ==‚Äì Be able to cook, but also be able to improvise with cooking.==
- ==**Map Route Memory**== ==‚Äì Underrated ability to know how to use a physical map! This will train their visuospatial working memory in a strong way.==

==‚ú®Ages 10‚Äì12: Pre-Abstract Thinking==

---

==**Main Goal:**== ==Develop visualization and sequencing for advanced math and geometry.==

### ==üß© Hands-on Building & Creating (Visuospatial Manipulation)==

==Strengthens ability to picture transformations in the mind.==

- ==**CAD Software or 3D Printer Models**== ==‚Äì Start working with advanced tools like CAD and 3D printers. Lots of libraries have these for free. Teach them to use blueprints but most importantly, teach them to make their own 3D designs and print. Reminder, they‚Äôll need to make a lot of mistakes to master.==
- ==**Make Blueprints and Build**== ==‚Äì 3D printers are cool, but they can build all sorts of things. Organization stuff around the house, their own tables, chairs, desks!==
- ==**Model Airplanes/Cars**== ==‚Äì Assemble from diagrams recalled in chunks. Anything in this zone is a good idea!==
- ==**Knitting/Crochet Patterns**== ==‚Äì Think more advanced patterns.==
- ==**Lego Architecture Sets**== ==‚Äì Assemble by recalling multiple pages. Lots of these can be combined with robotics so you get an engineering flair too.==
- ==**Optical Illusion Drawings**== ==‚Äì This is SO much fun and trains the brain in very unusual ways!==

### ==üîÑ Memory for Steps & Patterns (Spatial Sequencing)==

==Builds capacity for multi-step reasoning.==

- ==**Memory Mapping**== ==‚Äì Study a city map, redraw. Teach them memory palaces.==
- ==**Longer Dance Sequences**== ==‚Äì Memorizing entire dance sequences for 5-10 minute performances.==
- ==**Chess Replay**== ==‚Äì Same rules as above, but they should be getting better and better at this.==
- ==**Origami Series Recall**== ==‚Äì Completing structures with 10+ folds without notes.==
- ==**Sports Drill Sequences**== ==‚Äì Run multi-step plays from memory.==
- ==**Cooking from Memory**== ==‚Äì Prepare recipe without looking. Start to get to more complex meals with multiple steps, sauces, extras on top.==
- ==**Lab Experiment Steps**== ==‚Äì Don‚Äôt just teach them how to do experiments, but how to figure out how to do them on their own without steps. I have a post coming up on this.==
- ==**Theater Blocking Recall**== ==‚Äì Memorize stage positions while doing monologues for longer full plays.==
- ==**Video Game Map Paths**== ==‚Äì Navigate complex levels from recall.==

==‚ú®Ages 13‚Äì15: Abstract Problem-Solving==

---

==**Main Goal:**== ==Apply mental rotation and sequencing to abstract math and real-world tasks.==

### ==üß© Hands-on Building & Creating (Visuospatial Manipulation)==

- ==**3D CAD Replication**== ==‚Äì Advancing from above.==
- ==**Engineering Kits**== ==‚Äì There are a lot of advanced kits - be sure to encourage them to make their OWN things not just following instructions.==
- ==**Advanced Model Building or 3D printing**== ==‚Äì Large-scale builds in mental sections. Can they help you make something that you actually use around the house? Shelf organizers, hat holders, cup holders, etc.==
- ==**Architectural Drafting**== ==‚Äì Create floor plans and importantly BUILD it.==
- ==**Woodworking Projects**== ==‚Äì Making their own projects without instructions.==
- ==**Robotics Assembly**== ==‚Äì Recall wiring/placement order and do it themselves - and as always starting to make up their own.==
- ==**Art from Memory**== ==‚Äì Reproduce and make new art of increasing complexity==
- ==**Origami Tessellations**== ==‚Äì Large repeating patterns without notes - then encourage them to make up their own.==
- ==**Sculpture Copy**== ==‚Äì Study a piece, then recreate.==

### ==üîÑ Memory for Steps & Patterns (Spatial Sequencing)==

- ==**Algorithm building**== ==‚Äì Basic coding without assistance==
- ==**Long Chess Matches**== ==‚Äì Playing against harder players or memorizing plays from advanced players==
- ==**Advanced Dance or Martial Arts Forms**== ==‚Äì Recall without prompts.==
- ==**Orienteering Courses**== ==‚Äì Navigate long routes from memory or practice==
- ==**Complex Lab Protocols**== ==‚Äì Recall and reproduce multi-phase experiments.==
- ==**Multi-Layer Cooking**== ==‚Äì Bake recipes with many timed steps.==
- ==**DIY Project Steps**== ==‚Äì Build furniture without step-by-step instructions.==
- ==**Theater Scene Blocking**== ==‚Äì Full-scene movement recall.==
- ==**Group Choreography Creation**== ==‚Äì Design and remember complex routines.==

==‚ú®Ages 16‚Äì18: Expert Application==

---

==**Main Goal:**== ==Transfer visuospatial and sequencing skills into independent, real-world problem-solving - at these ages think about encouraging things that have real-world utility. It‚Äôs a great time to be building their resume!==

### ==üß© Hands-on Building & Creating (Visuospatial Manipulation)==

- ==**Architectural CAD Drafting**== ==‚Äì Produce 3D building renderings from site sketches. Use 3D printer to make and sell things people want online or to other kids.==
- ==**Medical Imaging Reconstruction**== ==‚Äì Recreate anatomical structures from scan slices.==
- ==**Industrial Robotics Prototyping**== ==‚Äì Assemble and program robotic arms from schematics.==
- ==**Mechanical Engineering Design Studio**== ==‚Äì Create functioning prototypes with minimal written plans.==
- ==**Set & Stage Design**== ==‚Äì Map, design, and build environments for theater/film productions.==
- ==**Furniture & Product Fabrication**== ==‚Äì Move from conceptual sketch to functional piece using CNC/3D printing.==
- ==**Ceramic Sculpture & Pottery**== ==‚Äì Shape large, balanced works from internalized 3D forms.==
- ==**Jewelry Design & Metalsmithing**== ==‚Äì Craft intricate multi-part pieces from sketches in memory.==
- ==**Fashion Draping & Patternmaking**== ==‚Äì Design garments directly on forms without full patterns.==
- ==**Woodworking Joinery**== ==‚Äì Build furniture with complex joints from mental blueprints.==
- ==**Game Environment Design (3D Modeling)**== ==‚Äì Create immersive spaces from concept sketches.==
- ==**Circuit Board Layout**== ==‚Äì Design and solder functional circuits from schematic recall.==

### ==üîÑ Memory for Steps & Patterns (Spatial Sequencing)==

- ==**Laboratory Research Protocols**== ==‚Äì Execute multi-step experiments without constantly checking notes.==
- ==**Software Development Sprints**== ==‚Äì Build modular codebases from pre-planned logic flows.==
- ==**Dance or Theater Direction**== ==‚Äì Stage and rehearse complex performances from memory.==
- ==**Urban Navigation Projects**== ==‚Äì Plan and test public transit routes without GPS reliance.==

- ==**Entrepreneurial Project Launch**== ==‚Äì Oversee multi-step product rollouts from concept to demo.==
