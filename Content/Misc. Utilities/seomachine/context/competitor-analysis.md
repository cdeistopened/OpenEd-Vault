# OpenEd Competitor Analysis

This document tracks key competitors in the homeschool curriculum, open education, and educational freedom space, analyzing their content strategies to identify opportunities for OpenEd.

**Last Updated**: December 2024

## Primary Competitors

### Competitor 1: The Good and the Beautiful

**Company Overview**:
- **Website**: https://www.goodandbeautiful.com
- **Primary Offering**: Complete homeschool curriculum (faith-based)
- **Target Audience**: Christian homeschool families, beginners through experienced
- **Pricing**: Free PDF downloads + paid physical materials
- **Market Position**: Leader in faith-based homeschool curriculum

**Content Strategy**:
- **Blog URL**: https://www.goodandbeautiful.com/blog
- **Publishing Frequency**: Regular, curriculum-focused
- **Content Types**: Curriculum guides, free resources, product pages
- **Content Quality**: High-quality, well-organized product pages

**Top Performing Pages** (highest traffic/rankings):
1. **/pages/free** - Traffic: 4,046/month
   - Keywords: "kindergarten homeschool curriculum", "2nd grade homeschool curriculum"
   - Why It Works: Free resources page captures high-intent searchers

2. **/pages/math** - Traffic: 2,862/month
   - Keywords: "homeschool math", "homeschool math curriculum"
   - Why It Works: Comprehensive subject page with clear grade progression

3. **/pages/language-arts** - Traffic: 2,649/month
   - Keywords: "language arts homeschool curriculum"
   - Why It Works: Subject pillar page targeting primary keyword

4. **/pages/handwriting** - Traffic: 1,619/month
   - Keywords: "homeschool handwriting curriculum"
   - Why It Works: Niche subject page with specific focus

5. **/pages/science** - Traffic: 1,464/month
   - Keywords: "homeschool science curriculum"
   - Why It Works: Subject pillar page

6. **/pages/pre-k** - Traffic: 1,262/month
   - Keywords: "preschool homeschool curriculum"
   - Why It Works: Grade-level targeting

7. **/pages/history** - Traffic: 1,168/month
   - Keywords: "history curriculum homeschool"
   - Why It Works: Subject pillar page

8. **/pages/high-school** - Traffic: 971/month
   - Keywords: "high school homeschool curriculum"
   - Why It Works: Grade-level targeting for underserved market

**SEO Strengths**:
- Strong domain authority from established brand
- Comprehensive grade-level and subject-level pages
- Free resources drive high organic traffic
- Clean URL structure and site architecture

**SEO Weaknesses**:
- Content focused on their curriculum, not educational philosophy
- Limited blog content on methods and approaches
- No content on funding, co-ops, or community aspects
- Doesn't address open education or mix-and-match approaches

**Content Gaps** (Topics they don't cover well):
- Educational methods beyond their curriculum (Charlotte Mason, Montessori, unschooling)
- State-specific homeschool requirements and funding
- Homeschool community and co-op guidance
- Learning differences and special needs approaches

**Differentiation Opportunities**:
- OpenEd can own the "how to choose" and "method comparison" content
- State-specific funding and requirements content is wide open
- Community and co-op content is underserved
- Mix-and-match philosophy vs. all-in-one curriculum approach

### Competitor 3: HSLDA (Home School Legal Defense Association)

**Company Overview**:
- **Website**: https://hslda.org
- **Primary Offering**: Legal defense, advocacy, homeschool requirements information
- **Target Audience**: All homeschool families seeking legal guidance
- **Market Position**: Authority on homeschool legality and requirements

**Content Strategy**:
- **Blog URL**: https://hslda.org/blog
- **Content Types**: Legal guides, state requirements, advocacy news
- **Content Quality**: Authoritative, comprehensive on legal topics

**Top Performing Content**:
- State law pages (rank for "[state] homeschool laws" queries)
- Requirements guides
- "How to start homeschooling" content

**SEO Strengths**:
- High domain authority as established nonprofit
- Definitive source for state-by-state legal requirements
- Strong backlink profile from educational institutions

**SEO Weaknesses**:
- Legal/advocacy focus, not practical curriculum guidance
- No content on methods, curriculum, or educational philosophy
- Dry, institutional tone

**Content Gaps**:
- Practical "how to actually homeschool" guidance
- Curriculum recommendations
- Community and social aspects
- Open education philosophy

**Differentiation Opportunities**:
- HSLDA owns legal, OpenEd can own practical implementation
- Link to HSLDA for legal requirements, provide value-add practical content
- Partner opportunity: HSLDA handles "can I legally homeschool?" - OpenEd handles "how do I actually do it?"

**Cathy Duffy Reviews** (cathyduffyreviews.com):

- Comprehensive database of curriculum ratings
- High domain authority for "[curriculum name] review" queries

- Multiple review aggregators compete for curriculum comparison traffic

## Competitive Content Patterns

### Common Topics All Competitors Cover:
1. **Curriculum by subject** (math, science, language arts)
   - Standard approach: Product/curriculum pages
   - OpenEd differentiation: Method-agnostic guides that help parents choose approach

2. **Curriculum by grade level**
   - Standard approach: Grade-specific curriculum pages
   - OpenEd differentiation: Include method comparisons and mix-and-match options

### Emerging Topics Some Competitors Cover:
- **Microschooling**: Limited coverage, growing interest
  - Opportunity: OpenEd hub page positions us as authority

- **Hybrid homeschooling**: Underserved across competitors
  - Opportunity: Content on blending approaches

### Unique Topics Only OpenEd Should Own:
- **Open education philosophy** - No competitors in this space
- **Mix and match approaches** - Unique OpenEd positioning
- **State funding for homeschool** - Limited quality content exists
- **Homeschool co-ops near me** - Local intent, high volume (1,900), low difficulty (4)

## OpenEd's Unique Advantages

- **Open Education Philosophy**: No competitors occupy this positioning
- **Mix-and-Match Approach**: Unique vs. curriculum providers' all-in-one models
- **State Funding Expertise**: Practical funding guidance is underserved
- **Community Focus**: Co-ops, connection, not just curriculum
- **Parent Empowerment Messaging**: Different tone than institutional competitors

**How to Leverage in Content**:
- Lead with philosophy and parent empowerment
- Provide method-agnostic curriculum guidance
- Include funding information in relevant content
- Emphasize community and support throughout
- Use conversational, empowering tone vs. corporate competitors

## Usage Guidelines

### When Planning Content:
1. Check what competitors rank for target keywords
2. Identify gaps in their coverage (methods, funding, community)
3. Plan content that serves parents better than product-focused competitors
4. Note Good and Beautiful's grade/subject structure as benchmark
5. Assess difficulty scores to prioritize winnable keywords

### When Writing Content:
1. Reference competitor articles for comprehensiveness
2. Add OpenEd's unique perspective (open education, mix-and-match)
3. Include funding and community angles competitors miss
4. Create superior resource through depth and practical guidance
5. Differentiate with empowering, conversational tone
