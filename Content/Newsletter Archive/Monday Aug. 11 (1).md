# ==**THOUGHT:**== ==AI founder ditches college prep for Montessori and curiosity====**TREND:**== ==Texas schools to blend wooden beads with AI algorithms====**TOOL:**== ==New tool guides students through problems instead of giving answersGPT architect goes analog==

==Ben Mann helped architect GPT-3 and co-founded Anthropic, the OpenAI competitor behind Claude. How is he preparing his 3-year-old for an AI future?==

==[Sending her to Montessori school and completely ignoring traditional academic prep.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?t=1334&v=WWoyWNhx2XU&feature=youtu.be&utm_campaign=14243626-OpenEd%20Daily&utm_source=hs_email&utm_medium=email&_hsenc=p2ANqtz-8rsLAjTOB2tPdVfhMEF2megWygKTlBLzIvEijBvFZkYdc7fhGfurDHVrfLUfr_iUsI2wdU)==

=="10, 20 years ago... maybe I would be trying to line her up for going to a top tier school and doing all the extracurriculars," Mann explains. "But at this point, I don't think any of it's going to matter."==

==Instead, he wants his daughters to be "happy and thoughtful and curious and kind."==

==The logic is simple: when facts become instantly accessible through AI, the ability to ask good questions becomes more valuable than memorizing answers.==

==Everyone's having the same debate: iPad kids vs. wooden toys. Touch grass or touch screens?==

==Matt Bateman== ==[asked](https://x.com/mbateman/status/1945914796043608433?utm_campaign=14243626-OpenEd%20Daily&utm_source=hs_email&utm_medium=email&_hsenc=p2ANqtz-8rsLAjTOB2tPdVfhMEF2megWygKTlBLzIvEijBvFZkYdc7fhGfurDHVrfLUfr_iUsI2wdU)== =="why not both?" The co-founder of the== ==[Guidepost Montessori](https://www.guidepostmontessori.com/?utm_campaign=14243626-OpenEd%20Daily&utm_source=hs_email&utm_medium=email&_hsenc=p2ANqtz-8rsLAjTOB2tPdVfhMEF2megWygKTlBLzIvEijBvFZkYdc7fhGfurDHVrfLUfr_iUsI2wdU)== ==network is launching== ==[Montessorium](https://montessorium.school/?utm_campaign=14243626-OpenEd%20Daily&utm_source=hs_email&utm_medium=email&_hsenc=p2ANqtz-8rsLAjTOB2tPdVfhMEF2megWygKTlBLzIvEijBvFZkYdc7fhGfurDHVrfLUfr_iUsI2wdU)====—a pilot program that combines century-old Montessori methods with cutting-edge AI learning platforms.==

==For the uninitiated, the Montessori method is an educational approach where kids choose their own activities, work at their own pace, and learn through hands-on materials (think mixed-age classrooms and teachers who guide rather than lecture).==

==Bateman's pilot will combine this with== ==[Alpha School's](https://alpha.school/?utm_campaign=14243626-OpenEd%20Daily&utm_source=hs_email&utm_medium=email&_hsenc=p2ANqtz-8rsLAjTOB2tPdVfhMEF2megWygKTlBLzIvEijBvFZkYdc7fhGfurDHVrfLUfr_iUsI2wdU)== ==platform that has students performing 2-3 grade levels ahead. Kids learn multiplication the Montessori way—manipulating actual beads to understand place value—then practice on laptops until they're fluent.==

==Just when you thought we'd run out of ways to add "-ly" to startup names, along comes== ==[Solvely.ai](https://solvely.ai/?utm_campaign=14243626-OpenEd%20Daily&utm_source=hs_email&utm_medium=email&_hsenc=p2ANqtz-8rsLAjTOB2tPdVfhMEF2megWygKTlBLzIvEijBvFZkYdc7fhGfurDHVrfLUfr_iUsI2wdU)====—an AI-powered homework assistant used by 5 million students across 120+ countries.==

==The app is powered by Claude, Anthropic's AI assistant (the same company Ben Mann co-founded).==

==**How it works:**== ==You take a picture of the problem you're working on, and it guides you through step-by-step rather than just giving you answers. One physics student went from 62% to a B average: "Instead of copying answers, I'm learning to solve problems on my own."==

==It also creates interactive visualizations—drag sliders and watch supply-demand curves shift in real time. Abstract concepts become something you can actually manipulate and understand.==
