==But what some call a massive market inefficiency, we call an opportunity. It‚Äôs just basic supply and demand:== ==**reduce the supply of workers, and the price (wage) rises.**==

==While millions of college graduates compete for oversaturated white-collar jobs, skilled trades offer immediate employment, zero debt, and often six-figure incomes within a few years.==

==In a traditional academic model, as Sal Khan explains, we group students by age and shepherd them at the same pace. A student gets a 75% on a test‚Äîmissing a quarter of the material‚Äîand the class simply moves on to the next subject.==

=="Imagine if we built homes that way," the Khan Academy founder muses.==

=="You have two weeks. Do what you can. Inspector shows up, it's 75% complete. Great! That's a C+. Start the second floor, then the third floor. And all of a sudden, that whole structure collapses."==

==The alternative? Mastery learning‚Äîwhere time is variable but competency is fixed. Students work until they truly master the material, building solid foundations before advancing.==

## ==**THE GREAT GRADE CHARADE**==

==A Tennessee student identified as "William A." graduated with a 3.4 GPA‚Äîsolidly in B+ territory. Just one problem: he couldn't read the words on his diploma. He couldn't even spell his own name.==

==Now== ==[he's suing the Clarksville Montgomery County School System](https://www.wwnytv.com/2025/03/02/former-student-sues-school-saying-he-graduated-with-34-gpa-couldnt-read/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=the-3-4-gpa-student-who-couldn-t-read)== ==for denying him the "free appropriate education" guaranteed under federal law while awarding him grades suggesting competence he never achieved.==

==We wish this was merely an isolated case of one student falling through cracks. But it‚Äôs not, and it exposes the fa√ßade of our entire grading system‚Äîwhere compliance, assignments completed, and attendance often count more than actual learning.==

==According to court documents, William (who has dyslexia) frequently used AI software to complete assignments.==

=="To write a paper," the ruling states, "William would dictate his topic using speech-to-text software, paste it into ChatGPT, which would generate a paper, then run that through Grammarly."==

==His teachers accepted these papers while apparently never addressing his inability to read.==

==To be honest, we have to give William props for the sheer ingenuity.== ==[Like Barbara Corcoran‚Äîthe real estate mogul who credits her dyslexia with fueling her ambition to prove she wasn't stupid](https://www.instagram.com/p/DHlhogaOdUW/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=the-3-4-gpa-student-who-couldn-t-read)====‚Äîthis young man's resourcefulness may well lead him to success despite the system's failures. But that doesn't excuse what happened.==

==The court determined that the school's "accommodations" didn't help William learn‚Äîthey simply "did the work for him."==

==How many other students are receiving passing grades‚Äîeven excellent grades‚Äîwithout mastering fundamental skills? And who bears responsibility when a student graduates without the ability to function in society?==

==The judge's ruling was clear: Despite William's 3.4 GPA, the school failed to provide an education "tailored to the student's needs." In other words, grades without learning are meaningless credentials.==
