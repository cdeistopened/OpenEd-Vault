## ==**Your brain can't multitask (but here's what it can do)**==

==Remember being told your brain can juggle multiple things at once? Turns out that's a myth.==

[==Cognitive scientist Megan Sumeracki explains==](https://www.cultofpedagogy.com/multitasking/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=108-what-happened-in-2012) ==we== ==_can_== ==multitask! But only when one activity is on autopilot (like walking while listening to a podcast). It‚Äôs when we attempt to engage in two activities requiring focused attention that we miss crucial parts of both (like trying to watch two movies simultaneously).==

==This matters because traditional classrooms often force kids to multitask without realizing it: Taking notes while listening to lectures. Reading slides while processing explanations. Even having a phone nearby (not using it, just nearby) creates a cognitive tax.==

[==Read more at Cult of Pedagogy==](https://www.cultofpedagogy.com/multitasking/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=108-what-happened-in-2012)==.==

==Something changed dramatically for young people in the early 2010s. The data is striking:==

- ==Depression and anxiety rose >50%==
- ==Academic achievement reversed decades of steady gains==
- ==Social connection and dating plummeted==

==So‚Ä¶ what happened? ==This isn't about demonizing technology (it‚Äôs here to stay). It's about recognizing that how we structure learning environments matters more than ever.==

## ==**Using rewards strategically**==

[![](https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/33ae21e0-3317-4a9c-8cb6-b1831fc2d1a8/Screenshot_2025-01-08_at_7.48.12_AM.png?t=1736351301)](https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/33ae21e0-3317-4a9c-8cb6-b1831fc2d1a8/Screenshot_2025-01-08_at_7.48.12_AM.png?t=1736351301)

[==Read the full tweet==](https://x.com/NielsHoven/status/1874913708025303190?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=108-what-happened-in-2012)

==**A common trap in education:**== ==Creating reward systems that frame learning as "the bad thing I need to get paid to do."==

==Instead, try a framework from education innovator Niels Hoven:==

==Use== ==_**temporary**_== ==rewards strategically when at least one of the following criteria applies:==

1. ==The activity becomes more fun with practice==
2. ==The skill becomes easier with time==
3. ==You're building initial momentum==

==**Real example**====: Temporary rewards for ski lessons until the natural thrill of skiing takes over.==

==Think of it as a jumpstart, not a bribe. The goal is to help them discover what naturally motivates them.==

==The best reward is discovering you don't need the reward anymore.==
