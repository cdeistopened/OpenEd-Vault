## ==**GRADE LESS, LEARN MORE**==

==Edutopia just published a== [==research roundup==](https://www.edutopia.org/article/why-teachers-should-grade-less-frequently/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=valedictorian-s-confession-that-s-it-the-empty-feeling-at-the-top) ==with nine compelling reasons that less is more when it comes to grading.==

==A few that caught our attention:==

==**Feedback gets ignored when it comes with a grade.**== ==A 2021 study found that when students see a grade before they see feedback, they're likely to ignore teacher comments entirely. Delay the grade, and students actually engage with how to improve.==

==**Grades create artificial endpoints.**== ==That A or C stamp signals "learning complete" ‚Äì even when it's just beginning. Creating more low-stakes practice opportunities and fewer high-stakes assessments helps students embrace the journey, not just the destination.==

==**Peer assessment accelerates growth.**== ==Students who evaluate their own or others' work exhibited "significantly better academic performance" across all age groups. It turns out, the act of grading itself is a powerful learning opportunity.==
