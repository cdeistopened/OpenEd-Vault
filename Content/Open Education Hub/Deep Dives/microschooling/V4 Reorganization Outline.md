### 5. THE DIVERSITY OF MICROSCHOOL MODELS (~600 words - NEW CONSOLIDATED SECTION)
**Purpose:** Show the VARIETY of approaches in scannable format
**Structure:** Brief intro, then bulleted/bolded list with 1-2 sentence descriptions

**Intro paragraph:**
"Ask nine microschool founders what their school looks like and you might get eleven different answers. That's not a bug—it's a feature. Unlike traditional schools trying to serve everyone, microschools succeed by serving specific niches. Here are some of the models you'll find:"

**Models to include (bulleted with bold headers):**

- **Nature-based microschools** (Scola, Wild Flower Community School) - 3+ hours outside daily, emphasis on outdoor play and exploration through all seasons

- **Van-based/Mobile microschools** (Connected Teens) - Education on wheels, field trips as curriculum, community-based learning

- **Classical/Academic microschools** - Traditional liberal arts curriculum in small setting

- **Faith-based microschools** - 25% of all microschools, integrating religious instruction and values

- **Special needs/Twice-exceptional** (Sphinx Academy) - Serving neurodivergent learners, kids who are simultaneously gifted and learning disabled

- **Project-based learning microschools** (Acton Academy) - Student-driven projects, no grades, Hero's Journey framework

- **Montessori microschools** (Wildflower Schools) - Child-led learning, hands-on materials, mixed-age classrooms

- **Sudbury/Democratic schools** - Students vote on rules and policies, self-directed learning, emphasis on autonomy

- **Life skills focused** (Life Skills Academy in Las Vegas) - Practical skills, career exploration, real-world application

- **Arts-integrated** (Positively Arts in Las Vegas) - Using performing arts to build independence and confidence

- **Hybrid/Tech-enabled** (KaiPod, Prenda) - Online curriculum with in-person facilitation and community

- **Farm-based microschools** - Learning through agriculture, animal care, sustainability

**Close with:** "The common thread? Each serves a specific community with a specific philosophy. Rather than trying to be all things to all people, they're laser-focused on what they do best."
