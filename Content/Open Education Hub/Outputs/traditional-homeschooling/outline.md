# Traditional Homeschooling (OpenEd Pathway Outline)

## Positioning Statement
- Traditional homeschooling within OpenEd: parent-designed core with flexible partnerships.
- Ideal for families seeking full control over pace, values, and environment while remaining open to mix-and-match supports.

## Learner & Family Fit
- Signals it works: desire for personalized pacing, strong family culture, willingness to curate curriculum.
- Watch-outs: parent burnout, limited peer interaction, compliance requirements.

## Core Principles to Highlight
1. Parent as lead designer and mentor.
2. Flexible schedules tied to child energy rhythms.
3. Mastery-based progression using living and digital resources.
4. Documentation habits for state requirements and funding.

## Template Structure (Sections for Full Page)
1. **Quick Start Map** – three sample weekly rhythms (single child, multi-age, working parent).
2. **Curriculum Mix** – spine + supplements table (language arts, math, science, humanities, electives).
3. **Community & Socialization** – co-ops, clubs, sports, service ideas.
4. **Assessment & Record Keeping** – mastery logs, portfolios, transcript builders.
5. **Funding & Resources** – ESAs, OpenEd grants, library partnerships, low-cost platforms.
6. **Success Stories** – brief case studies (different ages/needs).
7. **Next Steps CTA** – link to OpenEd Discover + matching consultant.
