# Microschooling (OpenEd Pathway Outline)

## Positioning Statement
- Small, relationship-rich learning communities blending personalized instruction with shared culture.
- Ideal for families seeking peer interaction, expert mentors, and flexibility beyond traditional schools.

## Learner & Family Fit
- Signals it works: child thrives in collaborative settings, parents want structure without bureaucracy.
- Watch-outs: tuition/funding logistics, leadership capacity, ensuring quality control.

## Core Principles to Highlight
1. Low student-to-mentor ratios enabling individualized mastery.
2. Hybrid calendars (2-4 days in community, remaining days at home/remote).
3. Competency-based progression; exhibitions replace grades.
4. Co-creation with families—feedback loops, governance roles.
