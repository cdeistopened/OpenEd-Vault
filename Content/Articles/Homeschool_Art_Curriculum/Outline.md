## Introduction (Target: ~100-150 words)
*   **Hook:** Acknowledge the shift from "craft time" to art as a rigorous, holistic discipline. It's not just about keeping busy; it's about cognitive development, emotional regulation, and historical understanding.
*   **The Parent's Role:** Address the "non-artist" parent anxiety immediately. You don't need to be Picasso to teach art; you need to be a facilitator.
*   **Authority:** Mention that this guide synthesizes research with "OpenEd Staff Picks" and real parent experiences from our community.
*   **Thesis:** Whether you want a messy, sensory-rich kindergarten experience or a college-prep portfolio for a high schooler, there is a curriculum model that fits.

## Types of Homeschool Art Curriculum
*   **Process Art vs. Product Art:**
    *   *Process:* Focus on the journey/sensory experience (best for early years). "Masterbuilder" philosophy.
    *   *Product:* Focus on the finished artifact and skill acquisition (best for older kids).
    *   *Blend:* The best curricula often start with technique (product) and end with exploration (process).
*   **Charlotte Mason & Picture Study:**
    *   The "Habit of Attention."
    *   Looking at art (Observation) -> Hiding it -> Describing it (Narration).
    *   Resource mentions: *Simply Charlotte Mason*, *Ambleside Online*.
*   **Unit Studies (Integration):**
    *   Art not as a silo, but as the "visual language of history" or science (STEAM).
    *   *Example:* Sewing Civil War costumes or drawing skeletal structures.
*   **Delivery Formats:**
    *   **Video-Based:** Outsourcing to experts (e.g., *Creating a Masterpiece*, *Art for Kids Hub*).
    *   **Subscription Boxes:** "Open-and-go" convenience (e.g., *Homeschool Art Box*).

## Kindergarten Homeschool Art Curriculum
*   **Developmental Focus:** Sensory and tactile exploration. Fine motor skills (clay, beading, cutting).
*   **Methodology:** "Symbolic" drawing (drawing what they know, not what they see).
*   **OpenEd Community Picks:**
    *   **Crafty Kids Jr. Club:** Highly recommended by staff (Heather Colburn) for a "club" feel.
    *   **My Zoo Box:** Great for animal lovers; combines crafts with zoology (low prep).
    *   **iCreate Art Box:** Mentioned by Ashley Teerlink as a solid "open and go" option.
*   **Parent Tip:** "Manager of the Mess." Focus on providing materials and stepping back.

## Elementary Homeschool Art Curriculum
*   **Developmental Focus (Grades 1-3):** Continued exploration, introduction to art history.
*   **Developmental Focus (Grades 4-6):** The "Crisis of Confidence." Kids want realism. If they can't draw a horse that looks like a horse, they quit.
*   **Staff Favorite:** **The Great Artist Program (K-6)**.
    *   *Why:* Endorsed by Jen Johnson and the team. Easy for parents (no prep), works for co-ops, combines art history with projects.
*   **Resource Spotlight:**
    *   **Art for Kids Hub:** (Wendy Robison rec) Essential for ad-hoc "how-to" videos (origami, specific characters). Free/Low-cost.
    *   **Yellow Spot Sun:** Excellent for history integration (e.g., "Architects of the Ancient World").
*   **Accessibility Note:** Mention the Slack thread about color blindness. Look for kits with labeled supplies or use digital tools (MacOS accessibility settings) to help color-blind students distinguish palettes.

## Middle School Homeschool Art Curriculum
*   **Focus:** Skill-Based Instruction & Realism.
*   **Curriculum Recommendations:**
    *   **Creating a Masterpiece:** Professional media (pastels, copper tooling). Sharon Hofer’s gentle but technical style.
    *   **Drawing with Realism:** Specific bridge for students moving from symbolic to realistic drawing.
*   **Niche Interests (The "Hook"):**
    *   **Anime/Manga:** Don't dismiss it. Use it to teach anatomy and storytelling. *Anime Art Academy* or Outschool classes.
    *   **Digital Art:** Introduction to *Procreate* and *Adobe*. This is a career skill.

## High School Homeschool Art and Art History Curriculum
*   **Track 1: The Generalist (Fine Arts Credit)**
    *   Goal: Appreciation and cultural literacy.
    *   **Resources:** *You ARE An ARTiST* (integrated with history/lit), *The Annotated Mona Lisa* (spine for history).
    *   **AP Art History:** For rigorous students (*UC Scout*, *One Schoolhouse*).
*   **Track 2: The Specialist (Portfolio Prep)**
    *   Goal: Art school admission.
    *   **Requirement:** Observational drawing (from life, not photos). 10-20 pieces.
    *   **Resources:** *Ashcan Art*, *New York Art Studio* (intensive portfolio prep), Outschool portfolio reviews.
    *   **Digital Proficiency:** Graphic design and product design electives (*SchoolhouseTeachers*).

### Logistics & Mess Management (The "Real Talk" Section)
*   **Organization:** The "Art Cart" method.
*   **Paper Taming:** The "Digital Archive" (photograph and recycle) vs. the "Binder System."
*   **Boundaries:** "No wet media in bedrooms."

### Grading & Transcripts
*   **How to Grade Creativity:** Use a rubric (Effort, Following Directions, Craftsmanship) to make it objective.
*   **Calculating Credits:** Carnegie Unit (120-180 hours).
    *   *Tip:* Count museum visits, sketching time, and YouTube tutorials (*Art for Kids Hub*) toward these hours.

### The Importance of Creativity (Why Bother?)
*   *Brief nod to the resource summary:*
    *   **Ken Robinson/Feynman:** Avoiding the "standardization trap."
    *   **"Create Before You Consume":** Using art to curb passive screen time.
    *   **3 C's:** Curiosity, Curation, Connectivity as future-proof skills.
