### New Sources (Just Provided)

**3. Project MUSE - English Majors Can't Read Study**
- Study of 85 English majors at two Midwestern universities
- Asked to read first 7 paragraphs of Dickens' *Bleak House*
- Results:
  - **58% were "problematic readers"** - couldn't understand the passage, wouldn't be able to read the novel
  - **38% were "competent readers"** - understood about half
  - **Only 5% were "proficient readers"** - could actually read complex texts
- These are ENGLISH MAJORS - students who should be the BEST readers
- Many relied on SparkNotes, skimming, oversimplifying
- Average ACT Reading score: 22.4 (only 60% of questions correct, "low-intermediate level")
- **Key insight:** "If we teachers in the university ignore our students' actual reading levels, we run the risk of passing out diplomas to students who have not mastered reading complex texts"

**4. The Atlantic - "America Is Sliding Toward Illiteracy"**
- 33% of eighth graders reading "below basic" - highest since 1992
- 40% of fourth graders reading "below basic" - highest since 2000
- Average ACT score: 19.4 - worst since 1990
- Bottom tenth of students doing WORSE than in 1971
- Top tenth doing fine - inequality growing
- $190 billion in COVID relief money largely wasted
- **Key insight:** "We are seeing what the lost decade in American education has wrought"

**5. Anchorage Daily News - "It's Not Just About Literacy. It's a Cognitive Decline"**
- Argues this goes beyond reading - it's cognitive capacity
- Connected to attention spans, critical thinking, ability to process complex information
- Links to smartphone/social media usage
- **Key insight:** Reading crisis = civilization crisis (connects to Andrea's point)

## Interconnections Between Sources

### Connection 1: Reading → Everything
**Andrea Fife's insight** ("Reading is at the center of everything") is PROVEN by:
- Atlantic article: Bottom 10% falling further behind = opportunity gap widening
- MUSE study: Even English majors can't read complex texts = career implications
- ADN cognitive decline piece: Reading failure = civilization failure

**The ripple effect:**
- Can't keep up in group chats with friends (social)
- Most careers close off (professional)
- Can't participate as informed citizens (civic)
- Family relationships strain (personal)
- Self-esteem collapses (psychological)

### Connection 2: The System is Broken
**William A. story** is the INDIVIDUAL case that represents:
- Atlantic's data: 33% of 8th graders below basic
- MUSE study: 58% of English majors can't read complex texts
- System optimized for throughput (passing students) not learning (mastery)

**The pattern:**
- Social promotion advances kids who didn't learn
- Grade inflation hides the problem
- Students graduate without real skills
- Diplomas become meaningless

### Connection 3: Bad Reform vs. Good Reform
**Education Next article** shows TWO paths:

**Bad Reform (what we're NOT advocating):**
- "Equitable grading" that removes ALL accountability
- Joe Feldman charging $114K to tell districts to stop grading anything
- Clark County: "things have gotten worse"
- No real mastery verification

**Good Reform (what we ARE celebrating):**
- Competency-based education with ACTUAL mastery demonstration
- "Mastered" or "Not Yet" instead of A-F
- Students progress at own pace BUT must prove learning
- SNHU, Finland, New Zealand examples

### Connection 4: The Solution Pattern
**What actually works** (consistent across all successful examples):
1. **Clear expectations** - students know what mastery looks like
2. **Individual pacing** - not locked to cohort born same year
3. **Multiple attempts** - "not yet" becomes mastery through work
4. **Real demonstration** - prove you can use it, not just memorize it
5. **Actual accountability** - can't advance without mastering

**This aligns with OpenEd identity:**
- "Your child's uniqueness isn't a problem. It's the point."
- "Students Are Not Standard" (core principle)
- "Mastery Over Measurement" (core principle)
- Parents as confident educational designers
