- Grounds criticism in practitioner voices (students, teachers) not ideology
- Most OpenEd-aligned: questions systems that profit from maintaining problems

## TOOL Segment: "When to Pull Your Kid" - LinkedIn Framework Selection

### Framework Analysis

Based on LinkedIn Swipe File review, here are the best-fit frameworks:

#### OPTION 1: "11 Signs You Are a ____ And Don't Even Know It" (Template #34)

**Structure:**
```
[Number] Signs Your Child's School Has Prioritized Passing Over Learning

1. [Sign 1]
2. [Sign 2]
3. [Sign 3]
[etc.]

[Closing question or CTA]
```

**Fit Assessment:** ✅ STRONG
- Natural list structure (perfect for "signs to watch for")
- Creates self-recognition moment ("oh no, that's my school")
- Invitation to comment creates engagement
- Easy to scan and share

**Execution Preview:**
```
5 Signs Your Child's School Has Prioritized Passing Over Learning:

1. Social promotion is official policy (kids advance regardless of mastery)
2. Grade inflation is rampant (everyone's on honor roll)
3. Teachers resist giving honest feedback ("we don't want to discourage them")
4. Standardized tests are dismissed as "just a number"
5. Your kid has good grades but can't demonstrate basic skills

If you're seeing these signs, you're not being overprotective. You're being appropriately protective.

What would you add to this list?
```

#### OPTION 2: "5 Reasons To ____" (Template #63)

**Structure:**
```
[Number] Reasons to [Action]:

1. [Reason 1 with brief explanation]
2. [Reason 2 with brief explanation]
3. [Reason 3 with brief explanation]
[etc.]
```

**Fit Assessment:** ✅ STRONG
- Directly actionable (gives permission + rationale)
- Each reason can include mini-story or example
- Reframes "pulling your kid" as responsible choice, not overreaction
- Addresses objections preemptively

**Execution Preview:**
```
5 Reasons to Consider Alternatives When Your School Prioritizes Grades Over Learning:

1. Grade inflation hides real problems. William A. had a 3.4 GPA. He couldn't read his diploma.

2. Social promotion policies guarantee some kids slip through. When teachers can't fail students, students get failed by passing.

3. "Equitable grading" reforms remove accountability. No late penalties, unlimited retakes—sounds compassionate, but who's checking if they're actually learning?

4. Your child only gets one chance at these years. You can't get third grade back to "do it right" later.

5. You're not being overprotective. You're responding to institutional failure with appropriate action.

What's keeping you from exploring alternatives?
```

#### OPTION 3: "3 Things XXX Wish They Could Tell XXX" (Template #41 & #83)

**Structure:**
```
Are you a {{XXX}}?
Do you know what {{XXX}} wishes they could tell you?

Here are [number] things they wish they could tell you:

1. {{XXX}}
2. {{XXX}}
3. {{XXX}}

Agree/Disagree... leave it in the comments.
```

**Fit Assessment:** ⚠️ MODERATE-STRONG
- Creates insider knowledge feeling
- Could frame as "3 things homeschoolers wish they could tell public school parents"
- Or "3 things teachers wish they could tell parents" (about broken systems)
- Requires finding authentic voice (can't make up what "they" say)

**Execution Preview:**
```
Are you a public school parent?
Do you know what homeschooling families wish they could tell you?

Here are 3 things they've learned that you might not hear otherwise:

1. The grades don't mean what you think they mean. (See: William A.)

2. "It's fine for now" turns into "how did we get here?" faster than you think. Every year of fake progress compounds.

3. You're not stuck. There are more options than you realize—and most are more affordable than you'd guess.

Agree/Disagree?
```

### RECOMMENDED FRAMEWORK: Option 1 ("Signs Your School...")

**Why this is the best fit:**

1. **Directly responsive to William A. story:** The newsletter opens with a kid who couldn't read but had good grades. This tool helps parents identify if THEIR school has the same problem.

2. **Creates urgency without panic:** Signs format helps parents assess situation rationally

3. **Empowers without prescribing:** Doesn't say "pull your kid immediately"—gives framework for decision-making

4. **High shareability:** Parents will tag other parents ("Does this sound familiar?")

5. **Engagement mechanism:** "What would you add?" invites practitioners (teachers, homeschoolers) to contribute

6. **Aligns with OpenEd beliefs:**
   - Trusts parents to make informed decisions
   - Questions institutional measures
   - Provides local, practical assessment tools
