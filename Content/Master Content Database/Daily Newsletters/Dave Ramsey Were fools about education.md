"We're fools about education," [**says Dave Ramsey**](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DI_v7FdJzZ3/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=dave-ramsey-we-re-fools-about-education), who's built a career helping people dig out from financial holes they should have never fallen into.

View this post on Instagram [A post shared by OpenEd (@openedhq)](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DI_v7FdJzZ3/?utm_source=ig_embed&utm_campaign=loading)

The college debate isn't yes or no‚Äîit's about value. As Ramsey puts it: "Think about what it is you're gonna get for what you pay. Is this in your budget? Am I getting quality for what I'm paying?"

The extremes of the debate are both wrong:

**Extreme #1:** "College is never worth it!"
**Extreme #2:** "College is always worth it at any cost!"

When the value proposition fails, it often fails spectacularly. Ramsey shares an example:

"I talked to a young lady from South Carolina who wanted to go to Ole Miss, which involves out-of-state tuition that triples her cost of college. I said, 'So why is it you want to go to Ole Miss?'‚Äú

The answer: ‚Äú'It's a pretty town.'"

His verdict? "When you go out of state, you are not paying for an increased value. You're paying for geography, which when it comes to education would be known as stupid."

Meanwhile, "a good aircraft mechanic, a good diesel mechanic make 100K, and a whole bunch of you with master's degrees in sociology making 28 grand."

"I can gain skills, make money, and get ahead at a younger age," he explains in [**a recent article by Kerry McDonald for the 74 Million**](https://www.the74million.org/article/from-apprenticeships-to-microcredentials-why-alternatives-to-college-are-gaining-popularity/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=dave-ramsey-we-re-fools-about-education).

McDonald spotlights both sides of the employer/employee equation: Mike Harris‚ÄîVanderbilt engineering alum turned HVAC company owner‚Äîactively seeks apprentices like Chase.
