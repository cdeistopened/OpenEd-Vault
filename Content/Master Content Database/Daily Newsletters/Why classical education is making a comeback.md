## Classical Education Explained

Classical education is having a moment.

Rick Hess reports that the [number of classical schools has doubled in the past decade](https://content.opened.co/e3t/Ctc/JA+113/d5pV-k04/VWv1-y4j50WQW7kYHMs2k8KqGW4vJbXQ5BxRnyN6Q02t03qgz0W95jsWP6lZ3mZW2_x74T8VfpVFW6Pr75m4bxR-9V-5npr8KzhnbW6g0dXd57tsqhW7C9Jw81s3PTYW7ssv3249TVPXW4327Yz7qZgGlW6-zhcQ5XP2fFW903w731WYX4-V3V_wK7nTnhZW5ZtV-x5d4CDWW6VLsML4Bk1VCVv8hB07N1v1mN3JHXsf6JSLyW8cLptF7XfvhGW2d4wVL3Sw8CcW3kK69L19nJzrW7Nf95v5pb6d-W6wv88N90ghT3W1qJ1KZ278XfDW5fZCRT8bZdz6W23n3_-4YpFlsW1QhWpW4LjQ3CW6y33N96nNVQBW5GjMxt61ZXrsW1Dlgl25wWxZqW5W189d345Ws8W6yyqWt2t1h-nW3kGB9f54Gt01W7fcRp01FXpKGf3H_BMx04), with hundreds opening since the pandemic alone. But *homeschoolers* have been at the forefront of this revival – mixing online courses, local co-ops, and traditional curricula in ways that exemplify what we call "open education."

So, what exactly *is* classical education? And why are families choosing it over more "modern" approaches?

Pinning down what makes education *classical* has proven difficult.

We see this as a feature rather than a bug—more variety means families can choose the approach that aligns with their specific goals.

But to understand the options, we first need to examine what families and educators are trying to recover from the past.

Whether you're ready to dive headlong into Latin or just want to escape the 50-minute subject blocks in favor of a more integrated approach, there are more options than ever (including some available at no cost through OpenEd.)

We did our best to untangle the definitions and highlight the best resources to get started, from free options to full programs.

### **Live Beats Asynchronous Learning**

We believe that asynchronous learning can be fantastic for disciplined, self-motivated learners who thrive on flexibility. But Stanford Online High School's data reveals a harsh truth: even gifted students had 30% dropout rates in self-paced courses, compared to only 2-3% attrition in live classes. Research shows [synchronous online courses hit 85-88% completion rates while self-paced MOOCs (Massive Open Online Courses) struggle to get even 10% of students to finish](https://content.opened.co/e3t/Ctc/JA+113/d5pV-k04/VWv1-y4j50WQW7kYHMs2k8KqGW4vJbXQ5BxRnyN6Q02qY5nR3bW50kH_H6lZ3mtW239HZH6gDNDfW3kVxpl98TgyyW375LlJ2vtsKPW1ct5yb60vV3vW2vBykv2DL1fVW1gbs_06wlx-CW2K9VJG3HGz8WN6DVCLDvmt42N4WR9KwhFH6RW5dxQgz73glBMW4QR4QJ6zn4LQW9lPnDv8hc2_jW2vlJ_j8KLjkMW7HlN9j16G80sW5fJgzN446gKyN8W7DCFF1VRFW5ctbq73wy92LW7xk4ZP6ZXVZsW5cfMw-7GqsMsW7kPSvQ3bfGPCW4bC64T5HclYnW2gqgyL1LByWtW6vJ7941WnvCzVD851v2j5W0rW2f4kC33LRX-YW77-7sq5mrQQPW4cRq0Z4nv7SYW7b2Qxl1cNmwfN9jtxMLKl5CzW3DDJYD732V_GW8Dfm-W2g-qQgW6cTVJB8ZPcMGf7hlr8n04). ## Parting Thought

The most effective learning method hasn't changed in hundreds of years: Watch. Practice. Create.

This apprenticeship model built civilization—observe a master, practice the skill, then create something original. It worked for Michelangelo. It worked for Mozart. It's how your kid learned to walk and ride a bike, and it's how they'll acquire more advanced skills too.

**Everything else is just scaffolding around this basic human learning process.**
