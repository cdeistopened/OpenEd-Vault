### üìä 2 TRENDS

**1. THE ADULT LITERACY CRISIS CREATES GENERATIONAL CYCLES - **The reading crisis doesn't end at graduation. According to the[** U.S. Department of Education**](https://nces.ed.gov/pubs2019/2019179.pdf?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=how-savvy-parents-are-tutoring-their-kids), **54% of American adults** read below a 6th-grade level.

This creates a vicious cycle: Parents who struggle with reading often can't help with homework, skip bedtime stories, and pass on reading anxiety. The[** National Assessment of Educational Progress**](https://www.nationsreportcard.gov/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=how-savvy-parents-are-tutoring-their-kids) shows **66% of 4th graders** now read below proficiency‚Äîand **70% never catch up** without intervention.

The pattern repeats: Parent avoids reading ‚Üí Child misses early literacy exposure ‚Üí Child struggles in school ‚Üí Becomes an adult who avoids reading. Breaking this cycle requires treating family literacy as interconnected, not isolated problems.

HIGH-DOSAGE TUTORING - **Harvard economist Roland Fryer's award-winning[** research on high-dosage tutoring**](https://www.nber.org/papers/w23792?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=how-savvy-parents-are-tutoring-their-kids) found that 130+ hours of small-group tutoring significantly improved outcomes‚Äîbut only when delivered in the right "dosage."
