## **LIONS HUNT IN SPRINTS**

"We're not meant to graze all day, right? We're meant to hunt like lions."

Naval Ravikant

Humans aren't cattle. So why do we structure education and work/study like we are?

[**Naval Ravikant points out the obvious**](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DHbf3-qAOLw/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=study-like-a-lion-not-like-a-cow): intellectual performance doesn't follow a linear 9-to-5 pattern. What matters is intensity, not duration. Quality, not quantity.

The most effective approach: **Train hard. Sprint. Rest. Reassess. Repeat.**

Stop grazing. Start hunting.

## **82% CAN'T DO MATH**

**BREAKING:** 82% of students in Newark can't do math at grade level. 70% can't read at grade level.

[**Educators call these results "disappointing,"**](https://www.wbgo.org/news/2025-02-19/nj-educators-ponder-next-moves-after-disappointing-test-scores?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=study-like-a-lion-not-like-a-cow) which is a bit like calling a tornado a "stiff breeze."

To be clear: There's nothing inherently wrong with a child developing at their own pace, mastering concepts when they're ready rather than when an arbitrary system dictates. Children aren't standardized, and their learning shouldn't be either.

The traditional education system itself created and enforces these grade-level standards. It's failing by its own metrics.

At what point do we acknowledge that our educational shortcomings are based less on funding problems than a misalignment with how children actually learn?
