### **The Power of Open Education**

[**In the latest podcast**](https://youtu.be/qW9sseQvzYg?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=the-weekly-rundown), Matt and Isaac dive deeper into the evolution of education, questioning whether traditional labels like "homeschooler" still capture the diverse learning landscapes families are creating.

**Key points:**

- Education today defies simple categorization, blending online and offline, structured and unstructured approaches
- The 'Eclectic' approach, as highlighted in [**Pam Barnhill's homeschooling podcast**](https://player.fm/series/homeschool-better-together-with-pam-barnhill/what-i-really-think-about-homeschool-methods?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=the-weekly-rundown), combines elements from various methods to create truly personalized curricula
- OpenEd aims to provide language and concepts that reflect this educational shift

One OpenEd student's message to Matt on LinkedIn perfectly illustrates this new reality:

"I'm a homeschooler enrolled in OpenEd (which partners with a public school). I also split-enroll in my local school for band class, and I'm pursuing a competency-based associate degree through Southern New Hampshire University."

### **What Parents and Kids Really Want**

Recent studies confirm the shift. Here's what we're learning:

- 48% of K-12 parents are "Open-minded," with 26% of those being "Customizers" who mix and match educational options ([**2024 Choose to Learn report**](https://tytonpartners.com/choose-to-learn-2024-press-release/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=tl-dr-tuesday))
- [**A Danish study**](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/loniatimagineif_pbl-projectbasedlearning-activity-7196757683188707329-5NHr?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=the-weekly-rundown) found that kids crave more creativity and hands-on learning:62% want creative use of technology
- 56% desire learning outside the classroom
- 51% want more hands-on, creative activities

**What do kids want less of? **Passive lectures, individual work, and standardized online learning programs.
