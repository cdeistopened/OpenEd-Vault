### Revised Clip 2: "Beyond School vs. Homeschool"

**Isaac (37:43):** "Anyone who's doing two or more [education approaches] at the same time has basically said they may have it open to varying degrees. They may just have it open a crack, right? But like the box is closed. You're all in one school all day, every day that is closed."

**Amir (14:53):** "We blame our kids for our issues. That's what's happening."

**Amir (39:23):** "My son goes to an alternative micro school in San Francisco, and he spends also a lot of time on OutSchool. He has multiple other memberships."

**Isaac (40:22):** "We don't live in a world where our entire existence is contained in one box and one set of networks. We have our friends that we only do sports stuff with. We have our work friends and our life over there."

**Amir (42:04):** "It's a better structural model for how humans actually are. You have these different parts to yourself, different interests, different identities and they're intersectional."

**Amir (39:52):** "Network schooling is something I'm playing with, but we share this vision of openness and integration of multiple different approaches, which are incredible."

**Isaac (41:43):** "I think we've crossed the chasm, especially since COVID, to the pragmatists who are like, 'I don't have any strong feeling about one being better or worse. I'm not doing this for some really strong ideological reason. I just know that my kid is miserable in this class or in this school. I just need something that's better.'"
