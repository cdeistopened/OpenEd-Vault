Basic Summary: Learning should be fluid and not restricted by time slots; if a child is interested in a topic, they should be allowed to explore it deeply, even for hours. Essential subjects like math, reading, and writing should still be incorporated daily, but traditional school structures should be avoided at home. Instead, families can engage in learning through exploration and activities during the day, reducing homework-related tensions and fostering quality family time.
