## **THE COLLEGE GRAD REVERSAL**

College graduates are struggling more than the general workforce for the first time inâ€¦ ever?

Since at least the 1800s, new technology has benefitted those with more education. Industrialization, computers, and robotics replaced manual labor while creating opportunities for knowledge workers with degrees.

What's different now? Derek Thompson writes in The Atlantic that recent college graduates face a 5.8% unemployment rate â€“ the worst relative gap compared to overall unemployment in 40 years.

New York Fed data reveals this historical shift comes from:

1. Post-pandemic tech industry contraction
2. College degrees providing diminishing returns since 2010
3. Entry-level knowledge work being automated

"The collective delusion surrounding the primacy of four-year degrees is tipping," says Mike Rowe, "and when it falls, it's going to go splat."

What matters now isn't the credential, but what you can actually do.

  

### ðŸ“Š TREND: THE SKILLS ACQUISITION REVOLUTION

## **HIGH SCHOOLERS LANDING $70,000 JOBS**

While colleges face declining enrollment, a different trend is emerging: high school juniors receiving job offers with $70,000 starting salaries.

The Wall Street Journal reports companies facing skilled worker shortages are going directly to high schools, recruiting students before they even consider college. One welding student described the experience as "like I'm an athlete getting all this attention from all these pro teams."

This shift marks a radical departure from the "college for all" mindset that has dominated educational guidance for decades. Key drivers behind this trend:

- **Critical labor shortages** in skilled trades that can't be outsourced
- **Astronomical college costs** prompting families to reconsider the ROI of four-year degrees
- **Industry-school partnerships** creating direct pipelines from classroom to career
- **Hands-on learning programs** that prepare students for immediate employment

The message is clear: for many young people, the most lucrative educational path might not run through a university campus but through specialized skills training that begins in high school.

## **FIND YOUR PATH WITHOUT DEBT**

"I want students to know you don't have to go to an Ivy League school. You don't have to go somewhere where you're coming out with $100,000, $200,000 of debt because of a college degree," says Rose Ybaben.

Her service, [Soaring2College](https://soaring2college.com/), helps families create personalized post-high school plans that don't default to expensive four-year degrees.

Rose's approach is different. She starts with ninth graders, opening dialogue years before decisions are rushed. Her motto: "Together we can do it."

What you get:

- Four-year high school roadmaps tailored to each student
- Exploration of educational, career, and volunteer opportunities
- Support for traditional and non-traditional paths

Two other free tools that complement Rose's approach:

[CareerOneStop Self-Assessments](https://www.careeronestop.org/ExploreCareers/Assessments/self-assessments.aspx) - Department of Labor tools that match interests to careers in under 30 minutes.

[O*NET Interest Profiler](https://www.mynextmove.org/explore/ip) - Shows which careers fit your interests with which education levels.
