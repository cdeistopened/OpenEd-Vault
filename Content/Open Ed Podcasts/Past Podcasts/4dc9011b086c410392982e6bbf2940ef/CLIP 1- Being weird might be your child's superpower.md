**Raw Clip Content:**  
TK: "Yes, your children will be weird. And that's a very good thing when the concept of normalcy has been defined by a whole set of indoctrinated values that aren't even yours in the first place. The goal of life isn't to conform to whatever society defines in your generation as normal. There are lots of ways to define normal that are actually very dangerous and very unhealthy. And so I think every parent ought to be asking themselves the following question. In what ways do I want to help cultivate my child's weirdness? In what ways do I want my child to be weird? In fact, if you're a parent and you're thinking about these questions, look at your life right now and ask yourself, in what ways am I weird to my own benefit? In what ways am I different from everyone else that actually allows me to create value for everyone else? And how do I want to pass that on to my children? So it's an important concern. I don't want to disparage it. It's a fair question. But I think sometimes we approach that question in a very one sided way. So I'll lead with that."
