**THE POWER OF BOREDOM**

When was the last time you let your child get truly bored?

In our rush to fill every moment with structured learning, we often miss the magic that happens when children have nothing they _have_ to do. That empty space is where genuine curiosity begins.

As Matt Bowman puts it: "Sometimes you need to let a child get bored enough to then wake up and say, 'I have nothing I have to do. What do I _want_ to do?'"

This is where you discover what truly interests them. When they're not being directed, what do they naturally gravitate toward? What questions do they ask? What problems do they try to solve?

The most valuable learning often happens not when we're teaching, but when we're observing what captures our children's attention in those unstructured moments.

Next time you feel the pressure to fill every hour with "educational activities," try stepping back. Create space for boredom. Then watch closely ‚Äì you might just discover your child's next passion.

### üìä TREND

**THE LESS-IS-MORE REVOLUTION**

The old approach to personalized education looked a lot like this: "Start at 8:30am, finish at 3pm, and cram in as much curriculum as possible."

Sound familiar? You're not alone.

One of the most surprising shifts we're seeing among successful homeschooling families is the counter-intuitive move toward _doing less_. Rather than recreating the bell schedule of traditional schools, they're embracing a more fluid approach.

As [a recent survey of personalized education families](https://www.joinprisma.com/blog/how-do-homeschoolers-get-into-college) revealed, children who spent more time on fewer subjects showed stronger mastery and engagement than those with packed schedules.

"Simplicity is the new sophistication," as Leonardo da Vinci reminded us centuries ago. Rather than marking success by how many textbooks you complete, today's most effective home educators measure by depth of understanding and genuine enthusiasm.

The trend is clear: Less curriculum, more exploration. Less instruction, more discovery. Less checking boxes, more following curiosity.

What could you remove from your schedule this week to make room for deeper learning?

**THE SACRED 20-MINUTE METHOD**

Feeling overwhelmed by your homeschool day? Try this surprisingly effective approach that several OpenEd families have reported transforming their experience:

**The 20-20-20 Method**

- 20 minutes of focused math
- 20 minutes of reading
- 20 minutes of writing
- One hour of free exploration between each

That's it. No six-hour structured days. No complex curriculum maps. Just focused learning followed by abundant time for children to process, explore, and apply what they've learned.

"Research shows that digital curriculum with just 20 minutes each of math, reading, and writing is sufficient," explains Matt Bowman. "Insert between each of those 20-minute blocks a one-hour recess."

The secret? The brain needs time to absorb information, and the body needs movement to prepare for the next learning session. When children return to focused learning after play, their minds are refreshed and ready.

Try this simplified approach for a week and notice: Are your children more engaged during the focused time? Does learning feel less like a battle? Are you seeing more self-directed exploration during the break times?

Sometimes the most powerful tool isn't adding more to your day‚Äîit's creating the right rhythm.

**Kairos** (KAI-ross): _noun_

From ancient Greek, meaning "the right, critical, or opportune moment." Unlike chronos (sequential time), kairos refers to the perfect moment for action.

In education, it's recognizing when a child is truly ready to learn something‚Äînot when the curriculum says they should be.
