# My Kids Missed 100+ Days of School

  

"Is that even allowed?"

That was my first question for Matt Bowman when he suggested I could pull my kids out of school for a couple weeks.

I didn‚Äôt ask "Would that be good for them?" or "How would that work?"

My first instinct was fear.

"This dude is trying to get my kids expelled." I thought.

Here's the thing‚ÄîI've always been a disruptor by nature. I never wanted to fit in any box. As a former college football player turned speaking coach, I've spent my career helping people break free from systems that don't serve them. After surviving suicide attempts and discovering my authentic voice, I've built a business around helping others turn their message into a movement. My TEDx talk has been viewed over 2 million times, and I work with executives, entrepreneurs, and leaders who want to communicate with conviction and create real change.

But when it came to my children's education, I was just like everyone else‚Äîfollowing rules without question, accepting that traditional school was the only option. Even though I often hated the way things were taught, I felt trapped by the system.

"Just try it out for a bit,‚Äù Matt suggested, ‚ÄúYou don't have to make a fork in the road decision right now. Just pull them out of school for a couple weeks and take them on some learning adventures and see how they go."

I couldn't wrap my mind around it. My paradigm was completely stuck. But that conversation planted a powerful question in my mind: What if there was a different way?

What if it wasn't career suicide or sabotage? What if it was actually one of the most powerful, empowering things we could do?

So we decided to test it. First we took the summer and said, "Let's pretend we're doing home education to see if we want to send them to school for the year."

The kids were going wild. They wanted to be in school. My oldest two wanted the social interaction, the schedule, the friends. But we stuck with the experiment.

We took trips across the country. We saw the Grand Canyon. We were learning so much more than they ever did in a classroom. Our kids got way smarter. They'd listen to me do sales calls in the car and were ready to run businesses.

It was amazing. But they still wanted to go back to school when summer ended.

So we let them.

## The Law of Exposure

What happened next illustrates the key principle every parent needs to understand ‚Äì the law of exposure: The mind when stretched never returns to its original form.

Now they were back at school, but they noticed the rigidity. They missed the adventure. They would see me going to Colorado for work and want to go.

Having been exposed to the idea, I knew what to say: "Well, you can just come."

"Really?"

"Yeah. My friend Matt said we can do whatever we want as long as we send the school a note."

So I pulled them out and took them on my business trips. We took extended vacations.

My kids missed over a hundred days of school that year. They still had good grades. They were still student council leaders. It worked out fine.

Why? I knew that they could catch up (they'd probably be messing around half the time in school anyway).

## What You Need to Know

Here's what most parents don't realize: You have more educational freedom than you think. Every state allows homeschooling. Most allow educational travel. Many support hybrid approaches where kids mix traditional school with other learning experiences.

The key is understanding your state's requirements. Some need more documentation than others, but none force you to keep your kids in a system that isn't working. Organizations like the Home School Legal Defense Association (HSLDA.org) can help you understand your specific state's laws.

Today, my kids have the entrepreneur bug. My daughter starts a new business every 90 days (and burns through our money just as fast). But here's what matters: They're expressed. They're learning. And most importantly, they see that they have a say in their future.

They've learned something no curriculum could teach‚Äîthey don't have to accept systems that don't serve them. They can create their own path.

Is it perfect? No. Is it messy? Absolutely. But it's ours.

Remember what I said earlier: "The mind when stretched never returns to its original form."

Once you see what's possible‚Äîonce you witness your child thriving outside the traditional box‚Äîyou can't unsee it. That's when real change happens.

Here's my challenge to you: Start with one question. The same question that changed everything for our family:

**"What if?‚Äù**

What if there was a different way for your child to learn? What if it wasn't educational suicide or sabotage? What if it was actually one of the most powerful, empowering things you could do?

You don't have to overhaul everything tomorrow. Try a week-long educational trip. Pull them out for a meaningful family experience. See how it goes.

But most importantly, trust yourself. You know your child better than any system ever will.

The question isn't whether you can do this. The question is: Are you ready to discover what you and your kids are truly capable of when you stop accepting limitations that don't actually exist?
