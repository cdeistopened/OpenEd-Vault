## **Your Best "In The Moment" Is Enough**

_"When we look back, was it our best? No, but did we try to do the best in those moments? Yes."_

Homeschool mom Tess Scholl nails something we all need to hear: The difference between "best possible" and "best in the moment" matters.

You're making hundreds of micro-decisions every day. Perfect isn't possible. But genuine effort, made consistently with love? That transforms lives.

  

#   
Potential Newsletter item

üí° THOUGHT  
The Hindsight Trap: Why Homeschool Parents Need More Self-Compassion  
Ever look back at yesterday's lesson and think, "I should have done that differently"? That's hindsight bias talking - our brain's sneaky way of making past decisions seem obvious when they weren't.  
Here's the truth: You're making dozens of micro-decisions every day in real-time. While reflection helps us grow, harsh self-judgment doesn't. As one homeschool mom recently shared in our podcast: "When we look back, was it our best? No, but did we try to do the best in those moments? Yes."  
This hits at something deeper: The difference between "best possible" and "best in the moment." You're not just teaching math and reading - you're juggling energy levels, emotional needs, unexpected interruptions, and real-world constraints.  
Give yourself the same grace you'd offer another parent. Because the reality is, perfect homeschooling doesn't exist. But genuine effort, made consistently with love? That's what transforms lives.

  

  

# Linkedin

Self-compassion is the secret weapon of successful homeschoolers.

A powerful insight from homeschool mom Tess Scholl: We often beat ourselves up looking back at our decisions, but in each micro-moment, we truly do our best with what we have.

The key is giving yourself permission to be okay with that.

This mindset shift is fundamental to success, not just in homeschooling, but in life:

Instead of spiraling into guilt about not being or doing enough, acknowledge that you made the best choice possible in that moment.

But self-compassion alone isn't enough. Community becomes the multiplier.

As your children age and face different challenges, having a supportive network becomes crucial. The beauty is how it works both ways - parents initially seek community for their kids, but end up finding deep connections themselves.

Tess discovered this firsthand. Her community blossomed after becoming a parent, finding like-minded people who helped both her and her children thrive.

The takeaway:  
Success in homeschooling (and life) isn't about perfection. It's about being kind to yourself in the moments that matter and surrounding yourself with people who get it.

Remember: Your best in the moment is enough. And you don't have to do it alone.

# Linkedin Post

  

The hidden cost of not listening to our children goes far beyond their childhood years.

When we dismiss our kids' voices, we create adults who don't believe they're worthy of having opinions or setting boundaries.

Think about it:

Every time we brush off a child's thoughts or feelings, we're sending a message that their voice doesn't matter.

This compounds over time, creating adults who struggle with the simple question: "What do you want?"

As Tess Scholl points out, establishing this foundation of self-worth in childhood is crucial. It translates into self-confidence and self-awareness later in life.

The key isn't just listening - it's teaching kids that they deserve to be heard. And when they aren't heard, giving them the tools to advocate for themselves.

Many adults today follow prescribed paths because it's easier than answering the fundamental question of what they truly want. They lack the confidence to trust their own desires.

The good news?

We can break this cycle by:

- Creating space for children's voices
- Validating their opinions
- Teaching them advocacy skills
- Showing them their thoughts have value

The foundation of self-worth we build in childhood becomes the confidence adults carry through life.

Remember: Every interaction with a child is either building or diminishing their sense of self-worth. Choose wisely.

Your children's future relationship with their own voice depends on how you treat it today.
