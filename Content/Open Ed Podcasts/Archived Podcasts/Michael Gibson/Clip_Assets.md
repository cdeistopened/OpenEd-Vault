## Clip 2: The Startup is the Real School

**Rationale:** This reframes the scary, risky startup as the superior educational choice, directly contrasting it with the "coddled," ineffective environment of traditional school. It speaks to the core OpenEd value of learning by doing and building real-world capability.

#### On-Screen Hook Options (First 3 Seconds):
*   A failed startup is more valuable than a business school degree.
*   School coddles you. A startup makes you confront reality.
*   Want to learn about yourself? Start a company.
*   The fastest way to expose your flaws and weaknesses.

#### Platform Caption (e.g., Instagram/TikTok):
> Forget the classroom. The ultimate education is building something from scratch. #Entrepreneurship #LearnByDoing #StartupLife

#### Edited Transcript:
**Michael Gibson:** The project of building a startup is fundamentally a project of self-knowledge... startups are going to expose all the flaws and weaknesses you have as a person. Let's say you're afraid of having difficult conversations. Well, your startup is going to require you to have them... You're going to learn a lot about yourself running a company that you might not learn if you just have a specific role at a big company... What you can't do is ignore them. The startup will magnify everything about you... whether the outcome is a billion-dollar company or you flame out after a year, what you gained during that time is going to make it all worth it, in a way that makes it better than business school or working at a company. You have to learn by doing. If you're just living in the world of being in school, you're not learning how to deal with urgency or high-stakes environments. You're coddled.

#### Edited Transcript:
**Michael Gibson:** The people who play it safe in established institutions don't set their own goals. They have some broad goal, like a degree, but that's filled in by other people. They're given assignments and told how to do them. You become someone who has a brittle dependence on that institution for how to live your life... Think about skiing. You can wait until 22 to get on the bunny slope, or you can start at 15. You're only going to be able to handle the black diamonds of adulthood if you start practicing now. I encourage parents, even if your children are in school, you have to find ways for them to manage risk and pursue their own curiosity. None of this adult supervision; you have to let them go. We have to rewild the American child.
