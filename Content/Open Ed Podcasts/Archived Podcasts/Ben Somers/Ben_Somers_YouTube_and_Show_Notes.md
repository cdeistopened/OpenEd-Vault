### Edited Cold Open Transcript:

**Ela Richmond:** What got you interested in education to begin with?

**Ben Somers:** When I was 11, I had this teacher who was really frustrated that I was kind of like a foot tapper and wiggler. We had to go see a psychiatrist and I went on 70 milligrams of Concerta when I weighed like a hundred pounds. That's a really large dose of methamphetamines.

Now as an adult looking back, to fit into this model of how you're supposed to learn, you're supposed to just load up a hundred pound, 11 year olds with tons of drugs. I'm like, that seems really crazy.

**Ela Richmond:** Did you in the moment know that it was wrong?

**Ben Somers:** For sure. When I was 11, I was aware that when I took this pill in the morning, my day looked way different.

**Ela Richmond:** The purpose of the pill was just to keep you paying attention?

**Ben Somers:** It was really about the other kids. It wasn't about me. I was disruptive and probably talked a lot and was running around all the time. So it was to make it easier for the teacher to deal with the classroom. In my town that year, almost a quarter of all the boys in the school were on stimulants.

**(Swoosh Effect / Cut)**

**Ben Somers:** I've been surprised at how often kids you would label as unmotivated totally morph into absolutely those things in the right circumstance. You ask some kid who can't memorize state capitals and they can list all 372 Pokemon and which ones were a part of the different leagues and memorize them perfectly and all their moves. This doesn't make sense...
