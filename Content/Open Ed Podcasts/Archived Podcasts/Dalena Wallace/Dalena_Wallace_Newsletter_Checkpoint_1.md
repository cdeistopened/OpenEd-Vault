## SEGMENT 1: "I'M NOT GIFTED AT TEACHING" (THOUGHT)

**Source:** Dalena Wallace Podcast
**Type:** THOUGHT (Contrarian take, educational philosophy)

### Angle Options

1. **The Permission Angle:** A 13-year homeschool veteran's confession frees other moms to admit what they already know - they don't have to be good at teaching to give their kids a great education.

2. **The Strategy Angle:** Knowing your weakness isn't failure - it's the first step to building something better. Dalena designed around her limits instead of fighting them.

3. **The Industry Critique:** The homeschool curriculum industry wants you to believe the right workbook will solve your problems. Dalena spent 13 years learning otherwise.

4. **The Reframe:** She's not "outsourcing" - she's curating thoughtfully, prayerfully, and intentionally.

**Recommended:** Option 1 (Permission) - Most emotionally resonant. Opens with confession, lands on relief.

### Structure: Hook / Substance / Insight

**Hook:** Dalena Wallace has homeschooled six kids for 13 years. Her confession? "I'm not gifted at teaching."

**Substance:** Most homeschool moms feel pressure to become great teachers. Dalena did the opposite - she admitted her weakness and built around it. Tutors on Tuesdays and Thursdays. Co-op on Wednesdays. Online curriculum that grades itself. She kept the days she's good at (Mondays and Fridays) and outsourced the rest.

**Insight:** The best homeschool mom isn't the one who does it all. She's the one who builds the village that does it better.

**Key Quote:** "I'm curating it thoughtfully, prayerfully, and intentionally based on our family's needs."

## SEGMENT 2: HYBRID HOMESCHOOLING IS RISING (TREND)

**Source:** Dalena Wallace Podcast + Kansas School Data
**Type:** TREND (Current development, data)

### Angle Options

1. **The Data Story:** Kansas public schools lost 5,000 students in five years. Wichita closed six schools last year. Four more coming. Something's replacing them - and it's not what you'd expect.

2. **The Movement:** Post-COVID, teachers started their own schools. Parents formed pods. What seemed like emergency measures became permanent alternatives. Now there's a whole network of hybrid and micro schools in Kansas.

3. **The Church Opportunity:** Churches have classrooms, playgrounds, and gyms sitting empty Monday through Friday. Dalena sees them as the future of neighborhood-scale education.

4. **The Consolidation Problem:** When districts close schools, kindergartners end up on two-hour bus rides. The centralization that's supposed to save money is crushing families.

**Recommended:** Option 1 (Data Story) into Option 3 (Church Opportunity) - Data creates urgency; church vision offers hope.

### Structure: Hook / Substance / Insight

**Hook:** Kansas public schools lost 5,000 students in five years. Wichita alone closed six schools last year - with four more coming.

**Substance:** Where are families going? Into a growing ecosystem of hybrid homeschools, micro schools, and co-ops. Dalena Wallace runs [WISE](https://wisetogether.org/) - a network connecting these schools across Wichita. Her dream: a school in every neighborhood, just like there's a church in every neighborhood. Churches already have the classrooms and playgrounds. They're just sitting empty during the week.

**Insight:** The infrastructure for the next education already exists. Someone just needs to unlock the doors.

**Key Quote:** "My dream for the future is that there's a school in every neighborhood, just like there's a church in every neighborhood."

## SEGMENT 3: CURRICULUM THAT DOES THE WORK (TOOL)

**Source:** Dalena Wallace Podcast
**Type:** TOOL (Practical resources)

### Angle Options

1. **The "Self-Grading" Filter:** Dalena's criteria for curriculum: Does it grade itself? Does it assign itself? Can I check progress without sitting next to them? If not, she moves on.

2. **The Curriculum Picks:** Teaching Textbooks (math), Veritas Press (history), The Good and the Beautiful (beautiful but too intensive). Real reviews from a mom who's tried them all.

3. **The "Find the People" Framework:** Her actual top recommendation isn't curriculum - it's people. Retired teachers. Homeschool graduates. Neighbors who can teach home ec. The village.

4. **The Network Tools:** [WISE](https://wisetogether.org/) for finding micro schools. [HERO](https://heartlandhero.org/) for Christian school founders. [Vela Education Fund](https://velaedfund.org/) for grants.

**Recommended:** Option 2 (Curriculum Picks) + Option 3 (Find the People) - Gives concrete tools while reinforcing the Thought angle.

### Structure: Hook / Substance / Insight

**Hook:** After 13 years of homeschooling, Dalena Wallace has one filter for curriculum: "Does it grade itself?"

**Substance:** Her picks: [Teaching Textbooks](https://www.teachingtextbooks.com/) for math (kids log in, watch the lesson, do the problems - mom checks progress online but never grades papers). [Veritas Press](https://veritaspress.com/) for classical history (great for high schoolers, didn't click for middle school). [The Good and the Beautiful](https://www.goodandbeautiful.com/) is gorgeous but parent-intensive - she couldn't keep up with six kids.

But her real recommendation? "Find the people." Retired teachers. Homeschool graduates who want experience. Neighbors who can teach sewing or baking. The curriculum industry wants you to believe the right workbook solves your problems. Dalena learned otherwise.

**Insight:** Good curriculum that requires all your time isn't actually good curriculum - not for a family like hers.
