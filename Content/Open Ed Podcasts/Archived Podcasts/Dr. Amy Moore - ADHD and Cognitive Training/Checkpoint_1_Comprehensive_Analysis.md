Traditional schools accommodate struggles (extra time, front of class). But homeschoolers can remediate the underlying cognitive skills. This builds actual competence and resilience, not dependency.

## SURPRISING POINTS (What Contradicts Common Belief)

1. **Attention Isn't the Problem**
Common belief: ADHD = can't focus
Reality: Working memory, processing speed, long-term memory are weaker. Attention is actually average.

2. **Defiance Isn't Willfulness**
Common belief: Kid says "no" = they're being difficult/rebellious
Reality: Task is too hard, amygdala hijacked prefrontal cortex, they literally cannot think. They're in neurological shutdown.

3. **Yelling Doesn't Create Compliance**
Common belief: Yelling makes kids listen better
Reality: Yelling triggers fight-or-flight → auditory exclusion. Kids' ears neurologically can't process language. They're not ignoring you—they can't hear you.

4. **Extra Time Lowers Motivation**
Common belief: Giving kids more time helps them succeed
Reality: Extra time is "a terrible accommodation" because it lowers motivation. Better to remediate skills.

5. **ADHD Isn't Just About Medication**
Common belief: ADHD kid → prescribe medication → problem solved
Reality: Emotion regulation, sensory needs, sleep, parent regulation, cognitive training are all critical. Medication alone is insufficient.

6. **Parent Regulation > Child Therapy**
Common belief: We need to fix the kid's behavior/emotions
Reality: Mirror neurons mean parent dysregulation becomes child dysregulation. Fixing the parent comes first.

7. **Sensory Needs Are Behavioral Foundation**
Common belief: Bedtime is just about sleep schedule
Reality: Sensory seeker vs. avoider needs are completely different (swing vs. quiet), and if unmet, kid won't sleep regardless of routine.

8. **Homeschoolers Can Remediate, Not Just Accommodate**
Common belief: Homeschoolers have to work around ADHD limitations
Reality: Without classroom constraints, you can actually fix underlying cognitive skills. Not just accommodate—remediate.
