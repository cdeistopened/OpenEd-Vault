# Checkpoint 1: Comprehensive Analysis

## 5 Big Ideas (Marketing Angles)

1.  **Purpose is a Process, Not a Destination**: Debunking the myth that purpose is a single "thing" you find. It's a developmental process of exploring self and world.
2.  **The Boredom Paradox**: Why purpose sometimes requires pushing through boredom and how that builds cognitive resilience in teens.
3.  **Start Young, Start Small**: You don't ask an 8-year-old "What is your purpose?" You ask "What matters to you?" and build from there.
4.  **The "Beyond-the-Self" Hack**: How connecting learning to a larger cause (even just helping family) unlocks hidden motivation and academic performance.
5.  **Art as the First Language of Purpose**: How creative expression allows children to articulate their inner self before they have the words for it.

## Surprising Points
- **Purpose can be boring**: We often equate purpose with constant passion/excitement. Heather argues that true purpose provides the resilience to endure the *boring* parts of the work.
- **College admissions hurt purpose**: The pressure to "package" oneself for college often forces students to fabricate a purpose, which they then have to unlearn to actually find themselves.
- **Don't ask "What is your purpose?"**: This question shuts kids down. Ask "What matters to you?" instead.
