## **Making Books Come Alive**

[In our latest podcast episode](https://opened.co/podcast/episode-014-stop-outsourcing-your-childs-education/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=060-you-don-t-need-to-find-the-right-curriculum&_bhlid=1de2eaf0779f79ed4525d3bf82bf6aaa801074fe), Matt Beaudreau ‚Äì founder of the [Apogee Strong schools network](https://apogeestrong.com/apogee-schools?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=060-you-don-t-need-to-find-the-right-curriculum&_bhlid=20dac13a5ecdbc359186e013454a723d01793c01) ‚Äì shared some brilliant insights on fostering a love for reading in children. Here's how to transform reading from a chore into an adventure:

1. **Model the behavior:** Let your kids see you enjoying books.
2. **Freedom of choice:** Let your kids pick books that interest them.
3. **Curate classics:** Introduce timeless stories with conversational potential.
4. **Engage in dialogue:** Ask thought-provoking questions about their reading:
- "What would you do if you were the main character?"
- "How would you have handled that situation differently?"
5. **Create scenarios:** Present enticing or challenging situations based on their reading and ask them to defend their choices.
6. **Make it a family affair:** Discuss books together, turning reading into a shared experience.

He emphasizes that most kids naturally love stories - our job is to maintain that "factory setting" of curiosity and excitement.

**Bonus tip:** Try audiobooks for long car rides or while doing chores. It's a great way to sneak in extra "reading" time!
