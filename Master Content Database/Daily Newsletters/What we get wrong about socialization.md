# The Socialization Question

For decades, the question *"But what about socialization?"* has been the ultimate trump card against homeschooling. The fear is that you'll raise "weird" kids who can't function in the real world.

But this question is based on a flawed premise: that the environment inside a traditional school is "normal."

Is it normal to spend 15,000 hours in an artificially age-segregated environment, where you only interact with people born in the same year as you? The real world doesn't work that way.

This week's deep dive explores why the "normal" schooling environment might be the weirdest social experiment of all, and how to confidently build a social world for your child that's both richer and more practical).

# Parting Thought

Parents don't always choose a particular form of schooling because it's best for their kids.

Sometimes they choose what feels safest for themselves.

"The natural parenting instinct is to think, ‘We're never quite measuring up to the Joneses next door,’" Matt observed [on the SmartPath podcast](https://youtu.be/fuydqbIVXZs?si=Ieqn_fa6OPn6FUJW).

"Every parent has insecurity around that comparison that by default, but no one criticizes you for just dropping your kids off at your local school."

Instead of building confidence in their ability to guide their child's education, parents transfer that confidence to an institution. The school becomes the expert, and the parent becomes the chauffeur.
