## **A BRIEF GUIDE TO BUILDING EDUCATIONAL MICRO-COMMUNITY**

In 2023, Eleyah Knight turned a 14-passenger van into a mobile classroom that transports teen learners to their 3-day-a-week lessons.

Her "Connected Teens" program is at the leading edge of a movement growing 220% faster than any other educational model: micro-schools and communities.

What makes these micro-communities effective? Smaller groups of students (8-12 in the case of Connected Teens) create an environment where trust flourishes and every voice matters. As Eleyah discovered, "The small group setting has been transformative. Students develop a strong sense of identity and confidence without the overwhelming influence of traditional settings."

### **Your Five-Step Roadmap**

**1. Define Your Vision** Document your educational values with precision. What specific gaps will you address? How structured will your approach be? Clarity on paper becomes clarity in practice.

**2. Gather Your Founding Families** Begin with 1-5 families who genuinely share your vision. Map out complementary strengths among parents. Someone understands math, another loves literature, a third offers space. Design your first 4-6 weeks as a deliberate experiment.

**3. Structure With Flexibility** Eleyah's model works because it provides both consistency and adaptation:

- Mondays: Cooking and nutrition
- Tuesdays: Coding and theater
- Fridays: Field trips with guest teachers

This pattern creates security while leaving room for emerging interests.

**4. Document Clear Agreements** Address potential friction points before they emerge. How will students participate? What will each family contribute? How will you handle expenses? Put it in writing and review regularly.

**5. Embrace Simple Beginnings** Most successful communities start in living rooms, not lecture halls. [**As microschool maven Dalena Wallace observed after watching her three-family gathering grow to sixteen**](https://opened.co/blog/a-beginners-guide-to-microschools-the-educational-trend-growing-faster-than-homeschooling/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=weekly-roundup-a-brief-guide-to-building-educational-micro-communities): "Starting in homes is the most cost-effective approach."
