## **READING RESOURCES BEYOND BASICS**

Yesterday we highlighted [**The University of Florida Literacy Institute (UFLI)**](https://ufli.education.ufl.edu/foundations/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=the-false-choice) toolkit, but there are dozens of great reading resources out there.

If you're looking for alternatives, consider these research-backed options:

- [**Systematic and Engaging Early Literacy (SEEL)**](https://education.byu.edu/seel?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=the-false-choice): Developed at BYU, SEEL combines explicit instruction with playful, engaging activities. One kindergarten teacher reported achieving a 100% grade-level literacy rate using this approach. The curriculum focuses on making reading practice natural and meaningful through interactive conversations and play-based learning.
- [**Teach Your Child to Read in 100 Easy Lessons**](https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/Teach-Your-Child-to-Read-in-100-Easy-Lessons/Siegfried-Engelmann/9780671631987?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=the-false-choice): A highly structured, direct instruction program that many parents find effective for teaching reading with just 20 minutes per day. The scripted lessons require no preparation and gradually introduce letter sounds, blending, and reading comprehension.
- [**All About Reading**](https://www.allaboutlearningpress.com/all-about-reading/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=the-false-choice): An Orton-Gillingham-based multisensory program with engaging activities and decodable readers designed to build confidence and fluency.
- [**Logic of English Foundations**](https://logicofenglish.com/pages/foundations?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=the-false-choice): A comprehensive program that integrates phonics, handwriting, and grammar through a multisensory approach suitable for various learning styles.
- [**The Reading Lesson**](https://www.readinglesson.com/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=the-false-choice): A streamlined, parent-friendly curriculum that many homeschoolers have used successfully to teach reading by age 5-6.

Remember that the best curriculum is the one that works for YOUR child and that you can implement consistently. Don't be afraid to switch approaches if your first choice isn't clicking.
