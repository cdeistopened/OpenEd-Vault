## **The Super Mario Effect**

One of our favorite parts of Fabrega‚Äôs book comes in the chapter on game-based learning, where she cites former NASA engineer and[** CrunchLabs**](https://www.crunchlabs.com/?tw_source=google&tw_adid=681842773585&tw_campaign=20790322200&gad_source=1&gclid=Cj0KCQiA7NO7BhDsARIsADg_hIZ_CKyOfUohpsbs3n3v6AB32IegUSdhXfmDcKpw6_KNXJjbWhX8jIAaAt1BEALw_wcB&utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=104-2025-the-year-learning-gets-personal) founder Mark Rober's fascinating experiment with 50,000 participants attempting a coding puzzle:

- **Group 1: **Lost points for failed attempts (27% success)
- **Group 2: **No penalties for trying (68% success, and 2.5 more attempts to solve the puzzle)

[](https://www.amazon.com/Learning-Game-Teaching-Themselves-Challenge/dp/1804090093?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=104-2025-the-year-learning-gets-personal)
Rober calls this the "Super Mario Effect" ‚Äì in the game, dying isn't a setback. You restart and try again, bringing your new knowledge with you. No permanent record of failures, just progress toward the goal.
