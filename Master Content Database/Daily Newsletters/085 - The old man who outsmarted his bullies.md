## **When Rewards Backfire**

[**A viral story from ‚ÄòPeaceful Parenting Coach‚Äô Luke Adsit**](https://x.com/Peaceful_Tips/status/1857453749767487655?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=085-the-old-man-who-outsmarted-his-bullies) (@peaceful_tips) perfectly captures what many of us get wrong about motivation

*{An old man was tired of kids mocking him on his daily walk.*
*So he offered them a dollar each to do it.*
*The next day, they eagerly showed up to earn their reward.*
*Then he offered 25 cents. They still came, though less enthusiastically.*
*Finally, he offered just a penny.*
*"That's not worth it!" the kids protested, and never bothered him again.}*

The lesson? What starts as *intrinsic* (the kids' genuine, if misguided, enjoyment) becomes *extrinsic* when we add rewards.

Remove the reward, the behavior disappears. Likewise, bribing kids with screen-time as a reward for reading teaches them that learning itself isn't worthwhile ‚Äì only the reward matters.

## **The Montessori Method, Simplified**

Montessori education can be summed up in a simple but radical idea: Kids are naturally wired to learn. The educator‚Äôs job is to set up the right environment and get out of the way.

Sounds great! But how do you do it?

Here's Keri Mae‚Äôs suggested 20-minute Montessori makeover:

First, **clear** **one corner of your home.**

Next, **add three learning options:**

1. Something they love (piano app, art supplies)
2. Something they *might* love (new books, building blocks)
3. Something that *stretches* them (chess set, coding game)
[](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DC4jPkDzaDV/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=085-the-old-man-who-outsmarted-his-bullies)
Then do the hardest part: Walk away. Don't teach. Don't suggest. Don't hover.

Just watch what they choose.

After a week:

1. Remove what they ignore
2. Double down on what they love
3. Add one new option

Keri tried a makerspace, and her kids ignored it. Then, she swapped in [**Simply Piano**](https://www.hellosimply.com/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=085-the-old-man-who-outsmarted-his-bullies), and her son played for hours.

Start small. Start with one corner. Start today.
