## **DIGITAL DESPAIR: THE GREAT INVERSION**

The happiness curve has flipped. Historically, well-being traced a U-shape through life‚Äîhigh in youth, low in midlife, rising again in old age. Now it peaks in retirement and bottoms out in adolescence.

[**Dartmouth economist David Blanchflower's research**](https://www.the74million.org/article/a-leading-economist-echoes-psychologists-warnings-against-screens/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=great-writers-think-first) reveals this inversion coinciding with the smartphone revolution. Between 2003 and 2022, young women with 4+ hours of daily screen time exploded from 8% to 61%. Simultaneously, daily mental distress tripled from 1993 levels.

The tech-happiness paradox creates a design challenge for educational freedom. We champion technological access while witnessing its apparent capacity to dismantle psychological well-being.
