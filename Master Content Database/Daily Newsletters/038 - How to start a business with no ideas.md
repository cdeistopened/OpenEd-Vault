## **How to Start a Business (with no ideas)**

**You don't need a groundbreaking idea to start a business. You just need to start.**

**I remember when OpenEd was just a glimmer in my eye. There was no clearly defined roadmap, just a driving feeling that we could do education better for our children. So **[**this recent Forbes article**](https://www.forbes.com/advisor/business/software/want-start-business-but-no-ideas/?utm_source=opened-daily.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=038-how-to-start-a-business-with-no-ideas&_bhlid=ba5d22173640f7eb7e5b3e62c3b5ed621f2fdebc)** titled *‚ÄòHow to Start a Business with No Ideas‚Äô* caught my eye, and I had to share some of the lessons that resonate most with me:**

1. **Be a problem detective. The best businesses solve pain points. What irks you daily?**
2. **Passion + skill = potential goldmine. What do you love? What are you good at? That intersection is your sweet spot.**
3. **Network like your business depends on it (because it does). Ideas spark from conversations.**
4. **MVP = Minimum Viable Product, not Most Valuable Player. Start small, iterate fast.**
5. **Feedback is your new best friend. Embrace it, even when it stings.**

**Remember: **Every expert was once a beginner. Successful leaders didn't start there‚Äîthey started by starting.
